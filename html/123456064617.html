Company Profile


EBA Engineering Consultants Limited and its wholly owned subsidiaries make up the EBA Group of Companies. Founded by Dr. Elmer Brooker in 1966, the firm has grown to become a multi-disciplinary engineering firm operating in 15 offices across western and northern Canada. EBA specializes in environmental, civil, geotechnical, transportation and materials engineering including a compliment of biological scientists with staff of approximately 350. EBA's head office is in Edmonton with regional offices in Calgary and Vancouver. EBA also operates other regional offices in Alberta, BC, the Northwest Territories, the Yukon, Alaska and Moscow, Russia.


The primary services offered by EBA are environmental engineering and auditing, civil and geotechnical engineering, geological engineering, materials engineering, and quality control testing throughout the natural resources, industrial and urban development sector, including owners, operators, developers, architects, design engineers, contractors, and government authorities.


EBA Engineering Consultants Limited, is a diversified environmental management consulting company. EBA provides specialist consulting services in waste management, environmental planning, independent project review, feasibility studies, master planning or policy formulation, compliance auditing, site assessments, contamination and remediation strategies, landfill design and operation, water quality studies, socio-economic and environmental impact assessments.


EBA's computer facilities include a broad range of state-of-the-art technologies for engineering analyses, project management, word processing, CADD (computer aided design and drafting), communications and laboratory data acquisition. Microcomputers, integrated through local area networks, are available for numerical modelling and other computation-intensive environmental evaluation tasks. A comprehensive computer library which includes commercially available software, as well as specialty programs developed in-house, is readily available to all staff through a convenient network system.


Communication between all offices and the most remote field sites is readily accomplished through advanced transmission systems. Records management of the firm's project files is accomplished through on-line world-wide communication via Internet and EBA's in-house data management system.


Environmental Consulting Services: environmental site assessments; site contamination assessment and delineation; remediation plans for contaminated sitesl; underground storage tank decommissioning; landfill siting, permitting and design; environmental permitting; environmental impact assessments; hazardous/nonhazardous waste management; environmental compliance auditing; environmental project management; environmental planning; liner installation; groundwater resources engineering; groundwater monitoring programs; surface water quality management; environmental geophysics; environmental research projects; land reclamation; soil surveys; project management.

Environmental Products and Services
Product Name:
Site assessment and soils and groundwater remediation.
EBA has conducted over 1,000 Phase I Environmental Site Assessments through western and northern Canada. These have included a diverse scope of sites ranging from vacant sites for proposed redevelopment as residential properties to multi-storey office buildings as part of real estate transactions. Applicable assessments include sampling and laboratory testing programs for lead based points, asbestos, urea formaldehyde and PCBs. Assessments are in general conformance with the requirements of the Canadian Mortgage and Housing Corporation to support refinancing or sale of the property.

As a result of past experience, specific requirements from our clients and the continuing development of this industry, EBA has developed a proprietary Phase I Environmental Site Assessment form based upon the requirements of several industrial and governmental guidelines.

Project work includes intrusive investigations to determine the existing soil and groundwater conditions (Phase II), further delineation of the extent and magnitude of soil and groundwater contamination (Phase III), site remediation planning and implementation (Phases IV and V) and site monitoring (Phase VI). Risk assessment and risk management are also included where appropriate.

Product Name:
Contamination and reclamation assessments for upstream oil and gas industries.
EBA conducts historical review (Phase I), initial soil sampling (Phase II) and detailed sampling (Phase III) of decommissioned well sites and facilities. EBA identifies contamination from various sources including flare pits and product tanks. They determine treatability of materials based on the concentrations of salts, metals, semi-volatile organics present and types of hydrocarbons. EBA generally recommends on-site land treatment or bioremediation cells. EBA provides cost estimates for the above and other remediation options such as thermal desorbers and oilfield reclamation plants. Where appropriate, EBA evaluates central treatment versus site treatment for operating fields.

EBA conducts reclamation site assessments, supervision of reclamation activities, and applications for reclamation certificates on well sites. Remedial measures are recommended where sites will not pass criteria and construction supervision is provided on subsoiling to prevent topsoil/subsoil mixing. Moreover, EBA monitors soils, and conducts technical feasibility studies and cost analysis, in order to design and install suitable remediation facilities and ongoing monitoring.

Product Name:
Engineering solutions to issues of landfill siting, investigation design, impact assessment, construction and monitoring.
The selection of optimum landfill location is chosen of the basis of "constraint mapping". Impact assessments are undertaken, which include, potential human health impacts, destruction of archaeological sites, impact on man-made infrastructures and impact on surface water.

In the area of health impact assessment, EBA utilizes and applies the Alberta Health Impact Step Matrix technique. EBA has applied this technique for varying
applications as a means of assessing potential impacts of a landfill upon workers, residents and visitors. It is a highly versatile technique that is applicable to a wide range of situations.

EBA utilizes the traditional tools for landfill investigation, drilling and sampling. EBA also supplements high quality samples with in situ testing, geophysics, test pitting and detailed aerial photographic interpretation.

The data that is obtained from investigation may be recorded in ESEBase GIS Database. This format was devised by EBA and is now an industry standard in Western Canada and parts of the United States.

EBA monitors landfill performance both during the operation of the landfill through to the closure and post-closure of the landfill.
