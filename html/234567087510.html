DAVID R. MARACLE/NATIVE EXPRESSIONS
LIL CROW NATIVE ART GALLERY & CAFÉ
STAGE RED PERFORMANCE COURTYARD - BIO

BUSINESS OWNER  sole proprietorship

David and Kimberly Maracle are proud owners of NATIVE EXPRESSIONS LIL CROW NATIVE ART GALLERY & CAFÉ
on the Bay and have had the land in operation since 2009. David himself has been an entrepreneurial spirit on the Territory since 1985. Come and explore their Tiny but mighty, unique, first nations gallery, cafe, gift shop & outdoor performing arts courtyard.

Their rustic Cabin entitled "LIL CROW CAFE & STAGE RED" is nestled along the beautiful shores of the Bay of Quinte, (formerly known as Mohawk Bay) located just east of Highway 49 on the Tyendinaga Mohawk Territory.

People from around the globe come to enjoy an organic Coffee, Espresso, Specialty Tea, or a Sweetbird Smoothie and take in the beauty of First Nations Art, Music and Gifts at its finest.

People can try the all day Brecky Wraps, or traditional foods they offer while sitting by a roaring fire in our 27 foot tipi. Outdoor events, concerts, meetings, even weddings can be scheduled to take place in our Stage Red Performing Arts Courtyard. an Outdoor Performance Stage and Bistro, with seating available for 400 people with lawn chair; the bistro seats 25 to 30 people and is great for more intimate meetings.
A showcase of summer concerts also takes place every season; bringing Award winning First Nations Musicians and Youth from the community to come together to share their talents on stage.

This First Nations Destination offers an atmosphere of warmth, peace and serenity, and a sharing place of rich culture. Over the years, many people have noted how at home and relaxed they feel when they are visiting. A First Nations Experience of high caliber, not to be duplicated anywhere in Ontario.

On the gallery side, they have only Hand Crafted, Hand Carved First Nations Art and unique one of a kind ART- carvings, pottery, moccasins and an in-depth collection of First Nations Music in all genresThese high end quality pieces are from many First Nations around the world.

David R. Maracle is the featured artist of the Gallery. He takes great pride in bringing forth his Mohawk Heritage by keeping the Authenticity of his own items he produces; from Ancient Medicine Wheels, Smudge bowls, Talking Feathers, Mandalas and Iroquoian Love Flutes, to hand crafted Tomahawks and Sacred Healing Gourds. Each treasured product is unique, one of a kind, hand-crafted, top quality and made with countless hours of love and care.

MOHAWK HERITAGE & CULTURAL DESTINATION

Stage Red Performance Courtyard is an outdoor venue on the water of the beautiful Bay of Quinte, located in the heart of Tyendinaga Mohawk Territory. It is now moving towards its fourth season of hosting top quality outdoor concerts featuring our First Nations Professional Musicians and Youth from our communities; Juno and Grammy Award winning artists of native and non-native descent also have graced the stage. The Tiny Cabin is nestled along what used to be called Mohawk Bay. It is located just a stone throw from the heritage site for the landing of the Mohawks in 1784. The Landing is an annual commemoration of the historic arrival of the Mohawks here on May 22, 1784, although archival cartographic maps show Iroquois settlement on the north shore of Lake Ontario more than 150 years prior.

Celebrated since 1929, "Mohawk Landing" is held on the Sunday closest to May 22.. Traditional dress and Iroquoian food also enhance this unique historical celebration. Tyendinaga, when translated in English, means "where the wood comes together." Of special significance, the name is derived from " Thayentiné:ka," Captain Joseph Brant's Mohawk name and reflects the belief that "our strength shall be in unity." David R. Maracle is the owner of this breathtaking property, and his wife Kimberly helps to manage the property and oversees and executes all business contracts, and scheduling.





MOHAWK HERITAGE & CULTURAL DESTINATION
THE ROYAL TOUCH

Make your way to the Gothic-style Christ Church, Her Majesty's Royal Chapel of the Mohawks, on South Church Road, just above Bayshore Road, AROUND THE CORNER FROM LIL CROW CAFÉ to see one of only six "Chapels Royal" outside the United Kingdom. Now designated a National Historical Site, the stone Church was built by the Mohawks in 1843 to replace a log structure built soon after 1784 landing of the first arrivals.
The Church is filled with Royal Gifts, testament to the long association of the Mohawks with the British Crown. On display is a triptych in the Mohawk language given by King George III, a Royal coat of arms given by King George V, a Bible presented by Queen Victoria.
The Church's most historic artefact-used only on ceremonial occasions-is the treasured Communion Silver set given by Queen Anne in 1711 as a symbol of Mohawk and British alliance.



DAVID R. MARACLE AND THE VISION

David R. Maracle, Bear Clan, named Tehenneiakwa;tarons (cutter of stone) has lived on this historical land for over 35 years and finally settled on the waterfront and has called it home for 20 years; a gift from his Father Karoniaktatjeh. Davids heart has always been about First Nations Rights, Culture and Language; being able to convey teachings through his carvings of stone and his musical abilities has been his lifetime goal and achievement.

David in recent years, had a vision about the land  he wanted to build a stage for First Nations Musicians and Artists who are in the business, to provide a place to perform in between Ottawa, Montreal and Toronto. David felt it was time that a place be created and dedicated to First Nations Musicians and youth who were interested in the Music and Arts path. David has used all of his savings and his personal funds to take on this project by himself, with no help from grants or sponsors to date. David is a professional Musician himself, having 14 albums to his credit. He is also a World Renown First Nations Sculptor and knows first hand the importance of guidance and encouragement for the Youth who grow up in his community of Tyendinaga.

He thoroughly believes that if the Youth have a place to be encouraged and groomed in the Culture, the Language, Music, and Arts, the Youth will have a better chance in staying on a good road of health and happiness for generations to come. During Davids youth, he had a very strong mentor in his father, who always encouraged him in his artistic path and accredits his Father and Mother for the support he needed to achieve his goals.

David feels by giving back to the community and the youth, he has completed that circle of life that he was taught to respect. Native Expressions and Stage Red Performance Courtyard is a tribute stage for the youth. This is a place for the youth to come and showcase themselves, a healthy environment for them to be encouraged by fellow First Nations Musicians and Artists, in front of their whole community who come out to support them. What better way to instill pride and dignity to our First Nations Youth; BY EXAMPLE.

The stage has also been extended to the other colors of our medicine wheel to make it a multi-cultural venue with a First Nations organic ambiance. It is truly a magical, organic place, where the Traditional knowledge, Culture, Music and Arts unfold on the grounds of this beautiful courtyard. David and Kimberly wanted to share this wonderful piece of land with everyone, right in the heart of the Peacemakers Territory, Tyendinaga Mohawk Land....

ORIGINALS IN STONE/BONE/MIXED MEDIA
David began his career as a carver/sculptor in 1985. Traveling to Pow Wows, craft shows and fine art shows helped to develop and create a unique lifestyle, which allowed his abilities as a sculptor to soar. David, now an acclaimed World-Renowned Stone Carver/Sculptor, has received many awards globally for his unique and detailed works of art. His most prestigious award given was at The Heard Museum in Scottsdale, Arizona in 2008. The influences of his First Nations-Mohawk Heritage are clearly depicted in his sculptures. This has been his main goal; to educate people about the First Nations-Mohawk culture and issues through his artwork.

His sculptures have touched the hearts of many people; private and corporate collectors alike throughout the world. Some of his more famous Curators include Nelson Mandela, Loretta Lynne, Dan Akroyd, and the Emperor of Japan. David is well-known for his realism in his carvings of Eagles, Elders & First Nations Heritage symbols.

A highlight of his carving career - taking 1st prize Blue Ribbon with his one of a kind 3 foot sculpture entitled "Gathering Our Minds" at the Prestigious Scottsdale Arizona "Heard Museum" in 2009. Davids sculptures have been featured on cheques across Canada at all major banks, and he has had many art installations in High End Galleries all around Canada. For further information on any of his recent sculptures, or to purchase for Corporate gifting, please contact us at 613-396-2767 or visit the website at www.Davidrmaracle.com for detailed pictures of his one of a kind Originals in Stone.
