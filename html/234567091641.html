Company Profile


StarFish Medical is Canada's largest Medical Device service provider of design, development, and manufacturing services. With facilities in Victoria BC and Toronto ON, we partner with innovative companies throughout North America to create and manufacture breakthrough products for a full range of medical specialty areas including Digital Health, Microfluidics, and Ultrasound.

We use our proprietary PathFinder process for medical device product definition, technical engineering and product development Our prototype and volume production are delivered in an ISO 13485 certified facility with FDA registration.

Our ISO 13485 consultants provide technical regulatory assistance for FDA and Health Canada submissions (including IEC 60601). QMS-in-a-Box solutions deliver an effective and affordable Quality Management System that establish a clear commitment to medical device regulatory compliance for client companies.

Unique applications:

Pyng Medical Corp. commercializes award-winning trauma and resuscitation products for emergency medical responder and hospital. Their FAST1® Intraosseous (IO) Infusion System is the first FDA-cleared IO system designed specifically for use in the sternum during adult and adolescent emergency intervention. When normal peripheral intravenous access fails, FAST1® helps emergency care providersin a hospital or during pre-hospital responsequickly establish vascular access and administer emergency fluids and medications safely and effectively through the bone marrow of the manubrium. For over 10 years the product has been widely used in military markets because of its rugged construction and portability.

With 10 years of military success, Pyng Medical Corp. wanted to re-energize the product line, and keep it ahead of marketplace competitors. Sometimes viewed as less intuitive and a bit scary for civilian emergency service personnel, the FAST1® was ready for a civilian update.

Pyng Medical Corp. approached StarFish Medical to create an easier to use and more effective version of their flagship product more in line with the civilian marketplace. Pyng Medical Corp. chose StarFish to leverage a diverse, experienced R&D team without having to expand their internal engineering department.


The Process:
Working together, StarFish Medical and Pyng Medical Corp. engaged with key military and civilian customers and end-users to identify the strengths and weaknesses about the FAST1® product. The team visited various military installations with senior medical training officers and battlefield medics and spent time shadowing civilian ground and airborne ambulance teams in the field.

The team also visited the civilian and military medicine conferences and tradeshows in order to evaluate the competitor landscape and meet with prospective Pyng customers.

Preliminary research was followed up with focus groups with emergency medicine thought-leaders to corroborate the initial findings from the field. An extensive report detailed the differences between military and civilian markets.
The insights gathered as part of the process helped the StarFish team to create a Product Definition for the next IO device including DFx (Design For x) goals.

Results:
Updating the military device for civilian appeal makes the FASTResponder a more useful device on the battle field. Less needles with a mechanical redesign that shields them in a transparent plastic shell creates easier to handle device and improves civilian end-user perceptions.

Reducing the size, weight, appearance and the force required for insertion allowed a more intuitive and ergonomic product appropriate for all civilian providers . The original force to operate the device is reduced making it easier to use by all.

The product weight is cut by almost 50%, which reduces the medics backpack burden.

Together, StarFish Medical and Pyng Medical Corp have taken the FASTResponder product through the requirements testing process.

Bottomline:
On June 27, 2013, Pyng Medical Corp. announced that FASTResponder, the companys next generation IO Infusion device, received the CE Mark in Europe and is cleared by the United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for marketing in the United States. The FASTResponder was also cleared by Health Canada.

The FASTResponder product has received initial positive input from medical professionals for being easy to learn and easy to use. This feedback indicates the possibility of Pyng Intraosseous Infusion products being used in a wider range of applications and markets. This market expansion opportunity is anticipated to enhance Pyng Medical Corp sales potential moving forward and the company is increasing marketing activity.

StarFish Medical capabilities used in this case study: User centered design, contextual user research, human centered design, vascular access device, mechanism redesign, FDA registration, Plastics, ergonomics and human factors, Design ID and brand audit, prototype testing with cadavers, rapid prototyping, rapid molding and tooling, supplier audit and identification, Emergency Medicine, Tactical Medical Applications, Military Medical Applications

Pyng CASE STUDY December 2013 Interview with Mark Hodge, President & CEO (MH):
Updating a classic medical device design for new markets brings new insights and savings

1. What new at Pyng? MH: Pyng has really changed its level of robustness in last 2-3 years. Im confident that Pyng is a new company-- on the move with more to come. StarFish was a big help to us on the new product design front. In fact, we used the unique design, ease of use, and robustness of the FASTResponder Sternal Intraosseous Device as a model for improving our entire product line.
2. How is the transformation going? MH: In addition to redesigning our products to meet the needs of medical professionals, weve been changing how we distribute our products. Were now emphasizing distributors with sales forces that have strong relationships with medical professionals. Our distributors now demonstrate products, allow trials, and really illustrate the benefits of Pyng medical devices. Improved videos, new collateral, and hands-on education sessions featuring our Chief Medical Officer, Al Moloff, (next at SOMA) all help us tell our product story better. Our website is also evolving with new material and information.
3. What is the early reaction so far? MH: We spent a lot of the time on FASTResponder. The uniqueness of the design is very well understood and appreciated by customers. In fact, weve received extremely positive initial reactions from potential users in the medical community on all of our new products. So, early reaction is going well.
4. What do your customers like best? MH: Were receiving acclaim in two primary areas. The first is potential users in the medical community get that we listened to them in coming up with new design. The second is ease-of-use. They also understand how Pyng products can make their lives simpler in saving lives.
5. Any quotes? MH: Sure. My favorites come from end-users like Nurses and EMS Personnel. They say things like Wow! (The FASTResponder is) exceptionally easy to use. Its very gratifying. They feel we listened to them and did something about it.
6. Whats next for Pyng? MH: Were poised to capture the value weve already created for customers, while at the same time we have an engine in place that I believe can rapidly and efficiently bring new products to market. This engine will be the key to even more significant growth 2-5 years out.
