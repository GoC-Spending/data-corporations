Company Profile


Knight Piésold is an international consulting company providing engineering and environmental services for the mining, power, water, transportation and construction sectors. With offices around the globe, our team of over 850 experienced professionals delivers high quality specialized services and innovative solutions that respect social, environmental and economic responsibilities.

Knight Piésold Ltd. is the Canadian operation and has offices in both Vancouver, British Colombia and North Bay, Ontario.

North Bay Office Information:
1650 Main Street West
North Bay, ON
P1B 8G5 CANADA
Phone: 705-476-2165
Managing Principal: Rob Mercer

International offices are located in Australia, Argentina, Botswana, Chile, China, Ghana, Namibia, Peru, Singapore, South Africa, Swaziland, USA, UK, Vietnam, and Zambia.

Knight Piésold Ltd. has managed and conducted numerous environmental impact assessments of mining developments in Canada, the United States and around the world. Experience includes the coordination of Socioeconomic and Environmental Impact Statements for the BC Environmental Act (BCEAA), the Canadian Environmental Assessment Act (CEAA), the US Environmental Policy Act (NEPA), the California Environmental Policy Act (CEPA) and assisting in acquiring all permits, licenses and authorizations.

Internationally, the company has experience with World Bank standards and environmental laws in many countries around the world, including Argentina, Australia, Chile, China, Czech Republic, Mexico, Peru, South Africa, and Turkey.

For more information please visit www.knightpiesold.com.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Engineering and Environmental Services
Knight Piésold engineers, scientists and technical staff offer comprehensive consulting engineering and environmental services throughout all stages of project development, including:

- Project identification and concept development
- Government affairs assistance, project finance
- Engineering pre-feasibility and feasibility studies
- Project planning, scheduling, and cost estimating
- Environmental and social baseline studies
- Social environmental impact assessments
- Hydrological and hydrogeological investigations
- Detailed engineering designs and specifications
- Tender documentation and evaluation
- Lender due diligence reviews and environmental assessments
- Contract administration and site supervision
- Quality control, quality assurance and inspections
- Project management

Service Name:
Mine Tailings and Waste Management
The effective, safe disposal of mining wastes presents technical and environmental challenges. Each project is unique and our approach is to tailor the design of every tailings and waste rock storage facility to provide economic solutions appropriate to the site conditions, while providing long-term security and environmental protection.

Knight Piésold has extensive experience in siting, design, permitting, construction support, operational monitoring and closure of tailings and waste rock storage facilities for a wide variety of climatic and seismic conditions throughout the world.

Service Name:
Heap Leach Pad Design
From relatively straight forward sites to those in physically and environmentally challenging locations, our experience in designing heap leach pads encompasses all locations and sizes and includes some of the largest heap leaching operations in the world. Expertise is provided in a variety of liner and solution collection systems, loading and solution management strategies including for high fines content ores and wet and cold regions applications.

Service Name:
Pipelines and Pumpstations
Pipelines are a common means to transport a fluid from one point to another. Whether water or slurry, above ground or below ground, we have experienced pipeline and specialized discipline engineers who can make the necessary layouts, configure the flow control equipment and operators, and prepare detailed designs, provide construction quality and engineering support, and commissioning services. Our experience includes steel, concrete, HDPE, fiberglass, plastic, wood and other pipeline materials. We routinely perform steady state and water hammer analyses, and perform more sophisticated CFD and other dynamic simulations when required. Our mechanical and geotechnical analyses techniques allow our design team to reduce expensive anchor and thrust blocks, to provide our clients with competitive cost results.

Most water projects, energy projects and often mining projects have a requirement for pumps, pumping systems and pump stations. We use the latest engineering techniques to optimize and size pumps and pump motors. Our expertise includes all type of pumps from small capacity to large capacity, from simple well pumps to a large pumped storage pumped turbine unit. We have experience in all ranges of flows and heads and in all climate conditions. Our life cycle and energy efficiency efforts include pump selection, high efficiency motor selection, energy and capacity analyses, variable speed consideration, and other aspects which can reduce both operation costs and wasted energy.

Our experience in mine slurry and water pipelines and pumpstations ranges from design, specification and construction supervision, including test monitoring, followed by operational assistance and performance monitoring. By tailoring pump and pipeline selection to a range of flows, pressures and material conditions anticipated over the project life, we provide cost-effective and environmentally sound slurry and water handling. Our extensive experience in the management and handling of slurry streams includes tailings, concentrates and cycloned sands.

Service Name:
Rock Mechanics and Geotechnics
Through sound rock engineering principles, we implement advanced, cost-effective investigation and analytical methods to evaluate the geomechanical characteristics of various complex geological and mining conditions. We design and manage site investigation programs, develop pit slope designs, conduct stability assessments and operational monitoring for active open pit slopes, and evaluate stabilization/support systems for underground mines. Micro-seismic monitoring of underground mines is one of our specialties.

Service Name:
Mine Water Management
We have designed and assisted with managing a large number of water management systems for mining projects around the world. In most cases, these are site wide, fully integrated systems that involve a thorough understanding of the site-specific hydrometeorology, surface water (hydrology) and groundwater (hydrogeology), waste characterization, environmental and permit limitations and client preferred operational philosophies. Probabilistic modeling is commonly adopted to confirm that all extreme scenarios are identified and addressed in order to meet sustainability objectives.

Service Name:
Mine Closure and Reclamation
Our integrated and multi-disciplined expertise in post-operational site security and risk management is used to develop closure plans for new and existing mining operations that are compatible with the surrounding environment, regulatory requirements and cost efficiency considerations. Our engineering design and construction experiences provide the expertise for accurate reclamation cost estimating and establishing appropriate surety amounts.

Service Name:
Environmental Management Plans and Monitoring
We develop Environmental Management Plans (EMPs) to comply with legal or procedural requirements, but also to assist with project acceptance by local communities and stakeholders. EMP components may range from water resources and sediment control to worker health and safety. We offer environmental and socio-economic parameter monitoring for all stages of a project life cycle, from pre-development through to post-closure.

Service Name:
Environmental Baseline Studies
A full complement of environmental specialists, with a comprehensive understanding of the physical, chemical and biological environments, conduct required baseline and specialized studies in the areas of aquatics, flora and fauna, hydrology, hydrogeology, meteorology, air quality, water quality, and geochemistry. Our detailed baseline characterization identifies ways to produce socially and environmentally sustainable projects and enhances the efficiency of the project permitting process.

Service Name:
Social and Environmental Impact Assessments
Our experience with Social and Environmental Impact Assessments (SEIAs) encompasses work on projects located in multiple jurisdictions and in socially and/or environmentally sensitive areas that may involve population displacement and resettlement, as well as potential impacts to rare or endangered species. The SEIAs are carried out to achieve project designs that maximize project benefits, reduce impacts, and achieve a fusion of development with environmental protection and sustainability.

Service Name:
Social Studies and Community Engagement
Social responsibility is fundamental to credible business practice. As such, our team of social specialists is highly experienced in the practical development and execution of social impact assessments, public consultation programs, community development plans, indigenous peoples development plans and resettlement action plans. Our collective expertise, with culturally unique perspectives, in social and environmental impact evaluations and management systems, promotes globally relevant best practice in social responsibility.

Service Name:
Auditing and Due Diligence
Third party audits of social and environmental programs can assist proponents to demonstrate regulatory compliance, eligibility for certification, and increased transparency for stakeholders. We have considerable experience offering clients specialized review and assessment of social and environmental performance. Additionally, by conducting social and environmental due diligence, we provide an analysis of potential risks associated with a given project, including compliance and stakeholder acceptability.

Service Name:
Greenhouse Gas Accounting
Climate change is a pressing issue of global significance. Likewise, solutions are multidimensional and demand innovative approaches. We provide climate change services to help clients meet a variety of organizational goals and legislative requirements. We evaluate and manage the risks and opportunities associated with climate change and offer a full range of services including GHG accounting and inventory, offset calculations, and reduction strategies.

Service Name:
Hydropower Development
Working in the hydropower industry for almost 90 years has established a portfolio of hundreds of small to large hydroelectric projects throughout the world. Our experience includes water storage projects, run-of-river projects and pumped storage projects. By providing a one-stop-shop for hydropower development, from resource identification and evaluation through to feasibility, project management, detailed engineering design and site supervision, we are in the forefront of international consultants in this field.

Service Name:
Wind Power
Our renewable energy specialists have developed particular expertise in wind-hydro and wind-diesel power systems. We advance wind energy projects from conception to completion by evaluating wind energy resources, carrying out all permitting, modeling, geotechnical and interconnection studies, conducting financial analyses, managing construction, and supervising commissioning. Furthermore, we offer due diligence reviews for all aspects of the projects, from resource evaluation to project design, and perform evaluations of wind power plants once operational.

Service Name:
Renewable Energy
With the increasing importance of renewable energy technologies such as wind-diesel, kinetic-diesel and wave-diesel, we have moved forward by investing our efforts in clean power generation. Other renewable energy sources for which we provide engineering and environmental services, from project identification and feasibility to permitting and detailed design, include solar power, hydro kinetics, wave and tidal power, biogas power, biomass power and geothermal power.

Service Name:
Energy Efficiency and Energy Audits
We seek to eliminate wasted energy usage by identifying root factors that consume the most energy and reduce overall system efficiency. Through systems engineering approaches, we target costly equipment, systems and control loops, and plan for the future to effectively transform operations into a leaner, more productive and effective business. We prioritize energy and capacity usage through initial and ongoing systems reviews and audits using improved energy and demand-side management.

Service Name:
Transmission Lines and Substations
From 10 kV distributions lines up to 500 kV transmission lines, we have completed assignments for our power and mining sector clients in some of the most remote locations and most challenging terrains on earth. We offer a full range of engineering and environmental services associated with the design and permitting of transmission lines and substations particularly with hydroelectric and mining developments.

Service Name:
Interconnection Studies
We have extensive experience in identifying the most viable interconnection solutions for power projects around the world, ranging in size from 1 MW to over 1,000 MW and step up transformers from 4 kV to 500 kV. We provide interconnection voltage optimization, Point-of-Interconnection (POI) location assessment, power system planning, capital cost estimate, power flow analysis, and consultations and negotiations for generator interconnection with the POI transmission line operator.

Service Name:
Thermal Power
For more than 80 years in the thermal power sector, we have been supporting our public and private sector clients on projects ranging in size from 10 MW to over 4,000 MW. We have become a world leader in the engineering of cogeneration systems, circulating water systems and cooling towers. We also have specialized knowledge in many aspects of thermal power stations, including environmental protection, ash disposal, heavy machinery foundations, chimney stacks and major engineering structures such as boiler houses.

Service Name:
Hydrology
For hundreds of projects requiring detailed hydrologic studies, we have been in the forefront of providing specialist technical expertise in data collection and analysis, hydraulic design, flood plain management, river basin planning, water quality issues, analytical and numerical modeling, erosion control and sedimentation. Our engineers and scientists are experienced with a variety of advanced data collection, analytical and numerical models, and techniques for collecting, interpreting and estimating hydrologic values.


Service Name:
Hydrogeology
Our hydrogeological professionals have extensive experience providing services ranging from planning and implementing groundwater investigations and monitoring programs to designing dewatering systems and applying groundwater protection measures. We are committed to applying practical engineering approaches while managing water quality and quantity and minimizing groundwater impacts. Our areas of expertise include: hydraulic testing and groundwater sampling, well installations, mine dewatering solutions and process water supply, groundwater monitoring, and seepage analysis.

Service Name:
Dam Engineering
Dams play an integral role in many projects relating to mining, water storage, power development, irrigation and flood control. We have comprehensive experience across all facets of dam engineering from investigation, analysis and design to construction specifications development and construction QA/QC. More specifically, our services include inspections and dam safety, geotechnical site investigations, hydrology and hydraulics design, dam site alternative studies, dam design, seismic risk assessment and stability, construction QA/QC and support and instrumentation monitoring.

Service Name:
Water Delivery
Our engineers conduct studies to conceptualize and design water conveyance infrastructure. When pumping systems are required, we optimize power consumption and improve overall hydraulics to reduce head losses. We prepare technical specifications and requirements for flow control, monitoring and pumping systems. Moreover, we support owners with tender document preparation, qualified design-build (EPC) contractor selection, contract management, and inspection and QA/QC services.

Service Name:
Construction QA/QC
Knight Piésold works with clients to see that their construction projects meet design requirements, project capital budgets and allotted schedules through effective construction management and technical supervision.

We recognize the need for dependable and reliable quality assurance and quality control
(QA/QC) programs to confirm that the technical specifications of each project are being met. From on-site management and laboratory testing to final reporting, our specialists apply comprehensive solutions to all construction phases of the project.

The scope of our construction services includes the following:
- Project management, quality assurance and construction supervision
- Earthworks quality control
- Materials acceptance criteria
- Laboratory testing
- Foundations inspections
- Concrete testing
- Geomembrane liner testing
- Concrete and bituminous pavement testing

Service Name:
Cold Regions Engineering
Cold regions provide unique engineering challenges for mine developers. Many mines are in remote locations, where overland access can be difficult and dangerous. Vast distances place severe limitations on the costs of bringing labour, equipment and supplies to site, and on the cost of transporting the products to market. Knight Piésold has a proven track record in designing sound and cost-effective engineering solutions for mines in areas as difficult and remote as Canada's North, Alaska and other cold regions throughout the world.

Harsh environments impose unique constraints on the engineering design of projects in cold regions. Climatic concerns include extreme cold, wind chill, icing, reduced visibility due to blowing snow and radiation fogs. Geotechnical concerns may include permafrost, solifluction and frost-heaving, saturated soils and a lack of granular materials. Water may be in short supply, as the annual precipitation is usually low and surface water sources are frozen for most of the year. Mine facilities, including tailings disposal systems, water storage dams, waste rock repositories, decant systems and heap leach systems, must all be designed to operate in these extreme winter conditions.

Knight Piésold has gained significant experience in cold regions engineering from the involvement in mining projects in Northern Canada and the U.S. In addition, Knight Piésold has extensive experience working for mines in colder climates at high altitudes in the mountainous regions around the world.

Knight Piésolds experience includes:

 Site investigation programs to investigate general soil, bedrock and permafrost conditions associated with infrastructure.
 Winter drilling, including locating and investigating water sources for drilling, and measures taken to preserve frozen core samples.
 Pipeline and pump designs - completed considering extreme cold temperatures and potential effects on pipeline and pump materials and performance and operation.
 Design and construction of infrastructure considering extreme cold temperatures, in particular, scheduling and timing of certain components of the construction and what materials to use.
 Tailings management in extreme cold conditions.
 Water storage ponds designed and constructed in freezing conditions using free draining sand and synthetic liners.


Service Name:
Data Management and Analysis (FULCRUM)
FULCRUM is a web-based data management and analysis system that ensures project connectivity and efficiency by integrating numerous functionalities into a simple, user-friendly interface. Designed for providing reliable and controllable data storage, FULCRUM allows for information sharing through a data warehouse containing a full suite of analysis and reporting tools. This enables teams to upload, access and analyze data in real time, and clients to access their data through a secure interface from anywhere.

With contributions from over two hundred of Knight Piésolds engineers, scientists and developers around the world, FULCRUM is continuously expanding to host a wide range of data types, and currently hosts modules for hydrology, climatology, geochemistry, geotechnical instrumentation and environmental analyses.

FULCRUM provides a safe and secure location for the following:
- Data processing, auditing and storage
- Review and quality control
- File management and document libraries
- Equipment inventory and data collection planning
- Data analysis, interpretation and reporting

Service Name:
Cold Regions Engineering
Cold regions provide unique engineering challenges for mine developers. Many mines are in remote locations, where overland access can be difficult and dangerous. Vast distances place severe limitations on the costs of bringing labour, equipment and supplies to site, and on the cost of transporting the products to market. Knight Piésold has a proven track record in designing sound and cost-effective engineering solutions for mines in areas as difficult and remote as Canada's North, Alaska and other cold regions throughout the world.

Harsh environments impose unique constraints on the engineering design of projects in cold regions. Climatic concerns include extreme cold, wind chill, icing, reduced visibility due to blowing snow and radiation fogs. Geotechnical concerns may include permafrost, solifluction and frost-heaving, saturated soils and a lack of granular materials. Water may be in short supply, as the annual precipitation is usually low and surface water sources are frozen for most of the year. Mine facilities, including tailings disposal systems, water storage dams, waste rock repositories, decant systems and heap leach systems, must all be designed to operate in these extreme winter conditions.

Knight Piésold has gained significant experience in cold regions engineering from the involvement in mining projects in Northern Canada and the U.S. In addition, Knight Piésold has extensive experience working for mines in colder climates at high altitudes in the mountainous regions around the world.

Knight Piésolds experience includes:

 Site investigation programs to investigate general soil, bedrock and permafrost conditions associated with infrastructure.
 Winter drilling, including locating and investigating water sources for drilling, and measures taken to preserve frozen core samples.
 Pipeline and pump designs - completed considering extreme cold temperatures and potential effects on pipeline and pump materials and performance and operation.
 Design and construction of infrastructure considering extreme cold temperatures, in particular, scheduling and timing of certain components of the construction and what materials to use.
 Tailings management in extreme cold conditions.
 Water storage ponds designed and constructed in freezing conditions using free draining sand and synthetic liners.

Designed for providing reliable and controllable data storage, FULCRUM allows for information sharing through a data warehouse containing a full suite of analysis and reporting tools. This enables teams to upload, access and analyze data in real time, and clients to access their data through a secure interface from anywhere.
