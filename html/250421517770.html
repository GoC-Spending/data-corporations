Thermidaire Corporation Canada Limited was established in Toronto, Canada, in 1946 as a manufacturer of environmentally friendly industrial water treatment chemicals, Approved by Agriculture Canada Food Inspection Agency The Company has since expanded and diversified its businesses and earned its share of the North American market.

Combining many years of experience with most up to date technology has enabled us to offer, reliable, cost effective, environmentally friendly, organic, boilers and cooling towers water Treatment programs, which cover every aspect with the efficient operation of steam, hot water systems and cooling towers. The programme will tackle existing problems and prevent them from recurring by supplying our organic, environmentally friendly water treatment, proven to be effective and safe.;

* THERMIDENE "A"

Description And Use

Thermidene "A" is a combustion catalyst reducing the facial surface tension of the oil resulting in a complete combustion, less carbon, soot, gums, etc.

Thermidene "A" absorbs the water collected from the tank sweating and condensate entrained in the air from the breather.

Treatment and Dosage

Initial dosage: 1 gallon of Thermidene "A" for each 1,000 gallons of oil in the tank.

Continuous dosage: 1 gallon of Thermidene "A" for each 3,000 gallons of oil in the tank.

If water is excessive, pump water from the bottom of the tank as much as possible, then add 1 gallon of Thermidene "A" for each 500 gallons of oil in the tank.

The best results are obtained when Thermidene "A" is put into the lines or storage tank just prior to delivery.

Benefits

Effective combustion booster.
Cleans the entire oil system of soot and slag deposits.

H.D. POWDER & LIQUID

DESCRIPTION AND USE

H.D. is a non-caustic, powder degreasing soluble in hot water, incorporating a blend of alkalis with suitable wettants.

H.D. is non-corrosive to ferrous and non-ferrous metals. Extra care should be given when cleaning aluminum and its alloys.

D.M STEAM BOILERS TREATMENT
STEAM BOILERS AND GENERATORS WATER TREATMENT

Based on a blend of carefully selected tannins for use in steam raising boilers and generators. provides both corrosion protection, scale inhibition, oxygen scavenging , forming a protective film and disrupting the crystal growth.

D.M. CONCENTRATE BOILER WATER TREATMENT FOR STEAM SYSTEMS

D.M. Concentrate is composed of several different organic tannins, both natural and synthetic. The formulation was carefully compounded to give the highest efficiency with all types of water in high, low and medium pressure boilers. It is also the ideal treatment for any residual hardness or residual oxygen, which may carry over from pre-treatment. The various components of D.M. Concentrate have been selected for their exceptional stability under all boiler conditions.

All the various organic components act in oxygen absorption, in forming a light flocculent sludge instead of scale, and in changing the oxide surface of all underwater ferrous substances to tannen/ferric complex. Some are better than others as oxygen scavengers. Some are better than others in forming sludge. Some in keeping it finely divided and flocculent. However, all work together as a team to produce the finest results. In many cases, one or two act as catalysts on each other. Blended together giving better results than the same amount of one ingredient alone.

D.M. Concentrate immediately acts on all dissolved and free oxygen in water, and in a matter of seconds all oxygen is absorbed.

The D.M. Concentrate does not act on dissolved solids. While they are in solution, they are harmless. However, as they come out of solution in the crystalline form and are in a position to form scale, the D.M. Concentrate as a colloidal suspension immediately coats the crystals as they start to form with a Monomolecular, non-adherent, colloidal "sheen" preventing crystalline growth and crystalline adherence by distorting the crystal shape, so that they cannot form scale, but only finely divided sludge which is removed with normal blow-down.

The D.M. Concentrate also chemically reacts with the underwater oxide surface of the various boiler tubes, boiler walls, and the tube sheets forming an inert tannate/ferric "sheen". This "sheen" gives absolutely no thermal insulation. However, by
covering the anode of the iron and changing the oxide cathodes to an inert tannate, existing galvanic corrosion is sealed off and future galvanic corrosion prevented, both by anodic and cathodic protection up to 2 Volts D.C.

The carefully calculated formulation of D.M. Concentrate and the interaction of its components render its action more effective than the action produced by any single tannin. Although D.M. Concentrate may appear to cost more per quart or gallon than other boiler treatments on the market, it must be remembered that D.M. is a CONCENTRATE. By manufacturing D.M. Concentrate ourselves, under our own control and supervision. The true test of the economy of a treatment is not the price per quart but how many thousand gallons of steam producing water that quart will treat. Also, how effectively it maintains the boiler in proper condition and eliminates continuous and costly down time and maintenance. The average dosage is up to 5 oz. per 1,000 gallons of make-up water.

SOME OF THE ADVANTAGES OF D.M. CONCENTRATE BOILER WATER TREATMENT ARE AS FOLLOWS:

1. D.M. Concentrate prevents hardness and other scale forming substances in solution or suspended matter entering the boiler with the feed water from adhering to the heated surfaces and a scale free boiler is maintained.

2. Silica, by the action of D.M. Concentrate is prevented from complexing with iron, alumina and hardness salts to form silicate scales.

3. Oxygen is absorbed in the boiler and pre-boiler parts of the system.

4. Active corrosion is arrested and incidence of further corrosion is prevented by the formation of the tannate/ferric oxide on underwater surfaces.

5. D.M. Concentrate reacts with any free iron in suspension to produce a tannate-iron oxide complex which counteracts its tendency to adhere to heated surfaces, particularly so when the surface has been changed by D.M. Concentrate to the tannate/ferric surface. This iron or iron oxide in suspension may have come from pick-up in the feed system or other systems external to the boiler.

6. The addition of D.M. Concentrate forming a colloidal suspension does not appreciably increase the total dissolved solids of the boiler water necessitating a greatly increased blow down. Over dosing of D.M. Concentrate can do no harm as the D.M. Concentrate in the boiler water is a reserve for future need, and this is merely increasing the reserve.

D.M. Concentrate will not carry over with steam.

Conventional chemical treatments usually based on phosphate and soda themselves contribute substantially to the total dissolved solids of the boiler water materially increasing the necessary blow down. They often change the boiler's action into not only being a boiler, but having to be a softening plant as well. The amount of alkalinity necessary to do the softening job, sometimes increases the T.D.S. and the alkalinity to the point where foaming and priming are likely -especially when sudden changes in the boiler load take place. With D.M., alkaline chemicals are only used to raise the pH to between 9.5 to 10 (this is below the T.D.S. and alkalinity limits, where priming or foaming is likely). They eliminate any possibility of acid corrosion and the D.M. Concentrate goes further and has greater oxygen absorption ability in alkaline waters. By using our conjunctional treatments with D.M., the amount of D.M. Concentrate used is slightly lowered.

7. D.M. Concentrate itself tends to inhibit caustic embrittlement. In any conjunctional treatment we supply for use with D.M. Concentrate in which there is caustic there is enough nitrate mixed with the caustic to insure that the caustic-nitrate ratio is above that needed to prevent caustic embrittlement.

8. D.M. Concentrate is non-toxic, non-caustic and is absolutely safe to handle and store. D.M. Concentrate is COMPLETELY
ENVIRONMENTALLY FRIENDLY.

9. Administration of the treatment is safe and simple. The testing procedure is positive and simple and is more easily and more quickly carried out by boiler room personnel.

10. The action of D.M. Concentrate in scale prevention is physical or mechanical - not chemical, with the result that D.M. Concentrate may be used to prevent scale formation in any type of water.

As the action of D.M. Concentrate on suspended Matter or dissolved solids as they come out of solution is mechanical, the D.M. Concentrate does not differentiate between silica and calcium sulphate for instance. It is equally efficient in protection against every kind of scale, from all types of water.

D.M. Concentrate has little effect on pH value. It is non-alkaline and about as acid as a "good cup of tea".

D.M. Concentrate can be used in boiler pressures where the temperature and pressure are high enough to render sodium sulphite unsafe, as sodium sulphite will, at these temperatures and pressures, possibly form sulphuric or sulphurous acid.

D.M. Concentrate can be, and should be, used after softening plants or any type of pre-treatment. The amount of D.M. Concentrate used will naturally be greatly lowered. The D.M. Concentrate reserve in the water is there when needed to treat any residual hardness or any residual oxygen that may come over due to any maloperation of the pre-treated plant or sudden increased demands above its designed flow rate.

APPLICATION OF NEUTRATHERM AND THERMAFILM

Let's have a close look at the properties, application and limitations of our two steam and return line corrosion control products.

NEUTRATHERM

This product is a 99% active dispersion of a complex alkaline amine. When fed to the boiler it volatizes over with the steam and condenses in the condensate, raising the pH above 7.0, thereby neutralizing the carbonic acid formed when carbon dioxide condenses with steam. This product protects against CARBON DIOXIDE CORROSION ONLY. If oxygen is present in the steam and condensate, pitting type corrosion can still occur, although the severity of oxygen attack is minimized at pH's above 7.0

Because Neutratherm is volatile and vaporizes at elevated temperatures, it should be fed through a chemical pot feeder as close to the boiler as possible. Avoid feeding to hot condensate tanks, or a good portion of the Neutratherm will be lost to atmosphere before reaching the boiler.

THERMAFILM

This product protects steam and condensate lines against BOTH carbon dioxide and oxygen corrosion. Thermafilm is a filming type amine.

Although this product is alkaline, it affords protection to the steam and return condensate system, not by raising pH but by forming impervious non-wettable film on the interior metal pipe walls. This creates a film-type barrier against the corrosive action of oxygen and carbon dioxide.

Thermafilm must be fed continuously and separately from other chemicals. It must be mixed in warm condensate, never above pH
9.0. Over-dosing must be avoided since this will cause the Octadecylamine to separate in the steam and "Gunk" out in the system as a sticky, gummy precipitate. Please refer all Thermafilm proposals to Head Office for review.

Both Thermafilm and Neutratherm have a highly detergent action in removing existing iron oxide deposits in lines and traps.

Initial dosages should be kept to a minimum when treating older systems.

SOME OF THE ADVANTAGES OF THERMITAN CONCENTRATE BOILER WATER TREATMENT ARE AS FOLLOWS:

1. THERMITAN Concentrate prevents hardness and other scale forming substances in solution or suspended matter entering the boiler
with the feed water from adhering to the heated surfaces and a scale free boiler is maintained.

2. Silica, by the action of THERMITAN Concentrate is prevented from complexing with iron, alumina and hardness salts to form
silicate scales.

3. Oxygen is absorbed in the boiler and pre-boiler parts of the system.

4. Active corrosion is arrested and incidence of further corrosion is prevented by the formation of the tannate/ferric
oxide on underwater surfaces.

5. THERMITAN Concentrate reacts with any free iron in suspension to produce a tannate-iron oxide complex which counteracts its
tendency to adhere to heated surfaces, particularly so when the surface has been changed by THERMITAN Concentrate to the tannate/ferric surface. This iron or iron oxide in suspension may have come from pick-up in the feed system or other systems external to the boiler.

6. The addition of THERMITAN Concentrate forming a colloidal suspension does not appreciably increase the total dissolved solids of the boiler water necessitating a greatly increased blow down. Over dosing of THERMITAN Concentrate can do no harm as the THERMITAN Concentrate in the boiler water is a reserve for future need, and this is merely increasing the reserve.

THERMITAN Concentrate will not carry over with steam.

Conventional chemical treatments usually based on phosphate and soda themselves contribute substantially to the total dissolved
solids of the boiler water materially increasing the necessary blow down. They often change the boiler's action into not only being a boiler, but having to be a softening plant as well. The amount of alkalinity necessary to do the softening job, sometimes increases the T.D.S. and the alkalinity to the point where foaming and priming are likely -especially when sudden changes in the boiler load take place. With THERMITAN., alkaline chemicals are only used to raise the pH to between 9.5 to 10 (this is below the T.D.S. and alkalinity limits, where priming or foaming is likely). They eliminate any possibility of acid corrosion and the THERMITAN Concentrate goes further and has greater oxygen absorption ability in alkaline waters.

By using our conjunctional treatments with THERMITAN., the amount of THERMITAN Concentrate used is slightly lowered.

7. THERMITAN Concentrate itself tends to inhibit caustic embrittlement. In any conjunctional treatment we supply for use with THERMITAN Concentrate in which there is caustic there is enough nitrate mixed with the caustic to insure that the caustic-
nitrate ratio is above that needed to prevent caustic embrittlement.

8. THERMITAN Concentrate is non-toxic, non-caustic and is absolutely safe to handle and store. THERMITAN is COMPLETELY
ENVIRONMENTALLY FRIENDLY.

9. Administration of the treatment is safe and simple. The testing procedure is positive and simple and is more easily and
more quickly carried out by boiler room personnel.

10. The action of THERMITAN Concentrate in scale prevention is physical or mechanical - not chemical, with the result that D.M.
Concentrate may be used to prevent scale formation in any type of water.

As the action of THERMITAN Concentrate on suspended Matter or dissolved solids as they come out of solution is mechanical, the
D.M. Concentrate does not differentiate between silica and calcium sulphate for instance. It is equally efficient in protection against every kind of scale, from all types of water.

THERMITAN Concentrate has little effect on pH value. It is non-alkaline and about as acid as a "good cup of tea".

THERMITAN Concentrate can be used in boiler pressures where the temperature and pressure are high enough to render sodium
sulphite unsafe, as sodium sulphite will, at these temperatures and pressures, possibly form sulphuric or sulphurous acid.

THERMITAN Concentrate can be, and should be, used after softening plants or any type of pre-treatment. The amount of THERMITAN
Concentrate used will naturally be greatly lowered. The THERMITAN. Concentrate reserve in the water is there when needed to treat
any residual hardness or any residual oxygen that may come over due to any maloperation of the pre-treated plant or sudden
increased demands above its designed flow rate.
