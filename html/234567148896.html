Company Profile


Outspoken Ally Consulting provides specialized training on issues surrounding societal diversity and minority peoples. The value that this specialized training provides is unique to each of our customer segments.
: For businesses and companies, Outspoken Ally creates and educated workforce that can attract and enhance the loyalty of both consumers and future employees who are diverse in culture, age, gender, and nationality.
: For schools and post-secondary institutions, Outspoken Ally creates informed cultures of social awareness by way of education on conflicting paradigms of diversity and inclusion.
: For organizations, NGO's, and unions, Outspoken Ally creates educated collectives that stand firm on equality for their diverse memberships, their families, and society at large.
Outspoken Ally Consulting provides a dynamic service built upon the disciplines of anthropology, sociology, philosophy, religious study, history, political science, and human rights. Working together with our client companies, organizations, educational institutions, and diverse social groups, we will create mass change.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Diversity Training
Canada has become a cultural kaleidoscope over the past few decades, and as this cultural diversity grows, many people are having difficulty keeping up. With different cultures come different customs and behaviours, and it is up to us to bridge the gap by respectfully forming relationships of trust and understanding. The diversity training provided by Outspoken Ally Consulting walks us through the history of attitudes toward difference in Canada, how those attitudes have changed, how they must continue to change, and where we seem to be headed. We discuss the three different types of minorities (visible, invisible, and cultural) and why we must be cognizant of the social discrimination against them. Finally, we discuss strategies for forming unity with minority communities in both our personal lives and in the workplace.

Service Name:
Diversity Training - LGBTQ
As violent crime declines in both Canada and the United States, violence against LGBTQ people is on the rise. This is due to an influx of anti-LGBTQ rhetoric and ideology that we must make ourselves fully aware of. With suicides and murders of LGBTQ people beginning to skyrocket, this is quite literally a matter of life and death. It is vitally important that training on this issue provide a complete understanding of it. Beginning with the values that Western civilization and society were founded on, we discuss where views against LGBTQ issues originated. Next, an exploration of how those ideas manifest themselves in our current culture, as well as why we are seeing an increase in the fight against equal rights for this particular minority. We then discuss statistics and stories regarding how these attitudes and manifestations directly affect those in the LGBTQ community. As with every topic covered, we must finally discuss the reasons for which we should take action and responsibility toward these issues, and what that action should look like in both our personal lives and in the workplace.

Service Name:
Diversity Training - Sex and Gender
This is an incredibly complex topic that was recently presented on by Outspoken Ally at St. Thomas University, in Fredericton NB. There is a lot of misinformation, resulting in much misunderstanding, about what sexual and gender identity actually are. Because of this, the session begins with explanations of these terms, and some theories around them. Next, the differences between sex and gender are explored, followed by a detailed discussion of the foundations for the social and internal conflicts surrounding them. For this particular issue, Outspoken Ally presents an ordered scale of pressures, moving from religious to individual. This scale demonstrates how conflict in regard to sexual and gender manifest and influence all of us day-to-day. Vitally important to this topic, as with all others, is to identify cultural manifestations of attitudes toward the issue(s), finishing with our social responsibilities - why we should care and what we can do to create unity and experience change.
