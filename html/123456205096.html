Company Profile


Incorporated - Nova Scotia, Canada in 1992.


Nu-Air designs and manufactures high quality Heat Recovery Ventilators (HRV's) and Air Exchangers for residential and commercial buildings. Models range in size from 90cfm (42 l/s) to 5000 cfm (2,272 l/s). In addition, we distribute a line of ventilation accessories including some proprietary products.

Advanced Integrated Mechancial Systems (AIMS) were added to our product offering in March 2002. AIMS systems combine space heating, hot water and continous ventilation with heat recovery, in an energy efficient integrated system, marked under the eKocomfort and Enerboss trrademarks.

Nu-Air is a certified production facility recognized by the Canadian Standards Association (CSA). We have taken steps towards ISO 9001 compliance with certification pending.


Our products have been successfully marketed in Canada, the USA, Japan, Scandinavia and other European countries. Our customers are based in the residential and light commercial new construction and renovation market sectors. Product distribution is through manufacturer's agents, wholesalers, retail building supply stores and ventilation contractors. Dealer inquiries are welcome.


Nu-Air is uncompromising in its efforts to address Heat Recovery and Indoor Air Quality issues. Quality is our watchword and we back our products with the industry's strongest warranties.


The company has established a presence on the World Wide Web Correspondence, including technical support, purchase orders, confirmations, and invoicing can be conducted via e-mail or traditional methods.

Product Name:
Heat Recovery Ventilator (HRV)
Residential & Commerical Applications
Heat recovery ventilators and air exchangers are designed for single and multi unit residential buildings. When installed and operated as specified, the system will exchange and filter air throughout the building while recovering energy used to heat or cool the building. Air quality problems associated with biological and chemical contaminants can be reduced or eliminated. Typical sources of air quality problems include: moisture, odours and infections from people; dander, saliva, feces and allergens from pets, insects and rodents; pollen and fungal growth from plants; moulds; dust mites and bacteria.

We also apply this technology to commercial buildings such as small office buildings, night clubs, gyms, swimming pools, and meeting halls.

About The System
The Enerboss is an advanced COMBO system Air Handler with
integrated Heat Recovery Ventilator. When coupled to a residential
boiler, domestic hot water heater or central boiler (multi-unit
buildings), the Enerboss provides space heating and ventilation to the
building. Order with the optional evaporator coil (not shown) and
couple to a compressor (split system) to meet cooling loads.
A General Electric ½ hp electrically commutated direct drive motor
handles circulation and ventilation. The energy-efficient blower is
designed to provide continuous ventilation at 65 cfm (30 l/s) and
circulating air at 350 cfm (165 l/s). The same blower also provides
fresh air to the heat recovery ventilation system. A separate blower
provides HRV exhaust. The system is designed for ventilation and air
collection from wet rooms.
The Enerboss features a compact design with simplified duct, water and electrical connections. Air filtration is an
important part of the Enerboss, with separate air filters for both circulating and ventilating air. Simple controls require only
a fan coil thermostat with a fan switch and optional dehumidistat.

 Advanced hot water coil designed for maximum

 ECM blower motor with constant CFM program
provides continuous air circulation with low
power draw.
 Aluminum cross flow HRV core.
 Independent HRV exhaust fan for dedicated wet
room, contaminated air collection without recirculation or short-circuiting.
 A unique exhaust air preheat frost prevention
strategy delivers warmer ventilation air to the
space and avoids recirculation of stale air. Using
heated water rather than heated air, this strategy
returns water to a heat generator at lower
temperatures for potential efficiency
improvements and does not detract from the
available space heating output.
 Compact, space saving design.

Product Name:
Energy Recovery Ventilator (ERV)
Residential & Commercial Applications
An energy-recovery ventilator, or ERV, is a special type of HRV that tempers the extremes of humidity in the incoming fresh air. Like a HRV, an ERV transfer heat between the two streams of air passing through the ERV core. But an ERV also transfers some of the moisture from the more humid stream of air to the drier stream of air.

In winter, when outdoor air is usually dry, an ERV increases the humidity of the incoming air, while in the summer, when outdoor air is usually more humid, an ERV can lower the humidity of the incoming air, as long as the house is air-conditioned. (In a house without air conditioning, the humidity levels of the indoor and outdoor air are essentially the same, so an ERV cant help lower humidity
ERVs are recommended for air-conditioned homes in hot, humid climates. In cold climates, where winter indoor air can be humid enough to cause window condensation, one of the main goals of a ventilation system is to lower indoor humidity levels. For that purpose, a HRV makes more sense than an ERV. For the same reason, ERVs are not recommended for pool or spa rooms, where HRVs are more appropriate.

An ERV core, sometimes called an enthalpic core, can be either a fixed core or a rotary-wheel core and is usually made of treated paper or polyester fiber. Rotary ERV cores are often impregnated with a desiccant to improve moisture transfer.
