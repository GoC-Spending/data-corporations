Company Profile


Sussex Telecom, 100% Canadian owned and operated, is part of the Sussex Communications Group, in business since 1988. The senior management of the Group has been involved in the telecommunications industry for many years, and has
extensive experience in the telecommunications sector.

The management of Sussex Communications Group pride themselves on being responsive and knowledgeable with respect to all aspects of the telecommunications business, and in providing a level of service that is economical, with a high degree of integrity.

Sussex believes that both the quality, reliability of our services, and the personal and customized attention provided to
our clients, are equally, or even more important, than the rates we negotiate. We strive to establish mutually beneficial long-
term partnerships with our clients. We truly understand and appreciate the importance of giving our clients the level of
service they expect from a professional, national telecommunications company.

We provide long distance services (Land Line and Cellular), Toll Free Numbers, Mobi-Fi, Calling Cards , HomePHONE and Business Lines, turnkey telephone systems,Teleconferencing, eFax via emails, and Stand Alone Voice Mail, to businesses & residences across Canada and internationally.

We also offer wholesale rates for Dedicated or Switched LD, Toll Free numbers and Calling Cards.

Technology profile

Sussex Telecom is completely automated for traffic handling as well as billing. STI operates five fully redundant processor platforms, located in an environmentally conditioned telecommunications facility, based at a secure location in Ontario.

The platforms and associated administrative modules are supplied by Cisco Systems, Phoenix Global Networks (now Telemedia Inc.), Symetrics Corp., ATS, and Dell Computer Corporation; software is based on Symetrics Corp, PGN, and ATS, and Microsoft, and has been further enhanced by the Sussex staff. The systems are capable of carrying in excess of 18,000,000 minutes/month of in/out
traffic, non-blocking.

The platforms consist of dual CPUs, full redundant mirrored hard disk drives, redundant power supplies, and state-
of-the-art control boards, all in industrial grade chassis. A spares inventory of critical components, such as CPU cards and
T1 interface cards, are kept on the premises; emergency power is provided from the standby UPS system, and is capable of providing 72 hours of uninterrupted power, in the event of a major power failure. Further unlimited backup is provided by the emergency generator system.

Access to the system is provided by a combination of local access lines and dedicated T1 facilities, and a dedicated wide bandwidth Ethernet link, to and from our network carriers. The network facilities are monitored around the clock. Outbound traffic is carried to the network carriers on additional dedicated T1 trunks, and via the Ethernet.

We also handle traffic coming through gateways located in Canada and other parts of the world. Our processors are capable of supporting PIN numbers up to 12 digits, two different languages as standard prompts (English, French,) and numerous custom language prompts, including prompts designed exclusively for your particular requirements
