Company Profile


Keefer Ecological Services Ltd. (KES) has been in operation since 2005, and is recognized for its work in ecological restoration, reclamation, research using native plants and environemntal assessments (baseline date collection, analysis & technical review). We work with a number of industrial and community-based clients throughout BC including mining, hydroelectric, and oil and gas companies, First Nations, municipalities, and others.
KES is composed of a group of dynamic professionals who excel in their chosen specialization, and are adaptable and innovative team players. Most importantly, our team prides itself on a high standard of professionalism, integrity, scientific rigor and the delivery of high quality results. Michael Keefer leads our team and is considered to be one of BCs foremost experts on indigenous plants and restoration ecology. He also has a passion for sustainability, which is reflected in our business operations.
KES is dedicated to all sizes of projects and is experienced in a range of Canadas ecosystems, including old-growth forests, wetlands and grasslands, as well as disturbance types of all sizes such as coal mines, burns, hydroelectric reservoirs, transmission lines, and power generation facilities.
KES is compliant with ISNetworld and PICS and we have a process of continual improvement with our safety management systems.
Over the last 5 years we have worked with over a dozen First Nation groups from all across British Columbia. Not only have positive relationships been established, they have also been nurtured. Continual partnerships and ongoing relationship building with First Nation groups are key facets to the success of the team.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
vegetation inventory
This service area includes many areas of plant ecology. These include: air photo interpretation, mapping, the inventory of plants (including SARA species), the vegetation of damaged sites, restoration of wetlands, grasslands and other habitat, ethnobotanical research and other areas.

Service Name:
ecological restoration
In our work we seek to rejuvinate natural systems in degraded landscapes through various ecological interventions. Many projects involve prescription development, native seed collection, propagation, planting and follow up monitoring.

Service Name:
training
Training is provided within the areas of the companies specialties. The training products include plant identification skills, ecology, native seed collections and ethnobotany. We partnered with Royal Roads University to develop and deliver the unique native plant horticulture training program called 'Growing Our Futures. Our team has worked in a wide number of communities to teach ecological restoration, plant identification, seed collection and other such related subjects.

Service Name:
research
We conceptualise and design research projects in the areas of botany, ecological restoration, ethnobotany and other related disciplines. Though we are a small business we have a strong professional network to make up for any possible gaps.

Service Name:
environmental assessment
KES has considerable experience in the field of environmental assessments (EA), both in the collection of data, analysis and technical reviews. Our core team along with our highly talented group of sub-contractors allows us to take on EA projects of all sizes. Our team has worked as technical review for EAs in the area of mine, hydroelectric, transmission lines, and others as a service provider to First Nations. In these technical reviews we seek to involve both the leadership, staff as well as the grassroots people in the community with the goal of building a strong understanding of the project as well as its potential impacts and benefits. For industry we have produced many baseline reports and have been actively involved in many stages of the process.

Service Name:
rare species recovery
Our team has worked in rare plant species recovery extensively for the last decade. Projects have included the writing of Federal Recovery Plans, translocations of populations, habitat restoration to support recovery, seed collections and other key aspects of recovery. Plants we have recently worked on include, whitebark pine (Pinus albicaulis), limber pine (P. flexilis), Spalding's campion (Silene spaldingii) and Parry's townsendia (Townsendia parryi).

Service Name:
environmental business development
Our team has worked on a number of initiatives to create new environmental businesses. Some examples are: Tipi Mountain Native Plants Ltd, Tipi Mountain Eco-Cultural Services Ltd. and Twin Sisters Native Plants Nursery. We have a number of ongoing projects which should see the creation of other new businesses.
