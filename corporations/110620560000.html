Company Profile


SNC-LAVALIN Environment

SNC-LAVALIN Environment Inc. (SLEI) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of the SNC-LAVALIN Group Inc. SNC-LAVALIN Inc. is Canadas largest engineering-construction company, and one of the most prominent in the world. Head quartered in Montreal, Quebec, SNC-LAVALIN Inc has more than 30 permanent offices worldwide and ongoing projects in more than 100 countries. Founded in 1911, SNC-LAVALIN Inc. has built a solid reputation in traditional engineering and, over the years, has broadened its services to provide total solutions to clients, regardless of location, industry or complexity. Its wealth of expertise is drawn from a professional core of some 8,800 employees and its corporate revenues in 2001 exceeded $ 2.3 billion CDN.

SNC-LAVALIN Environment Inc. has a wealth of experience dating from 1937 in the field of geotechnical engineering and 1973 in
the field of environmental sciences. SLEI offers consulting services in the following sector of activities: environmental studies, environmental planning and capacity development, geographic information systems, contaminated sites, waste-management, mining activities, pollution control, geotechnical engineering and agricultural and rural development.

SNC-LAVALIN Environment Inc. multidisciplinary team of professionals includes civil engineers specialized in water,
wastewater and storm water management and infrastructure, in geotechnical engineering, and mining engineering; chemists,
biologists, geologists, geographers, designers, meteorologists, oceanographers, hydrogeologists, cartographers, agronomists and
natural resource managers; and specialists in environmental planning, public consultation, social sciences and law. SLEI is
backed by one of the best equipped and most modern soil and materials laboratories in Canada.

SNC-LAVALIN Environment Inc. has a strong and enviable track record and serves a diverse clientele including small, medium
and large companies, all levels of government, domestic and international financial institutions and development banks. In
all its projects, SNC-LAVALIN Environment Inc. provides its clients with services of the highest quality. The firm is ISO
9001 certified and has a comprehensive quality assurance program.

SNC-LAVALIN Environment has been active on the international scene since the mid-seventies. It has successfully completed
projects in North and South America, Western and Eastern Europe, the Middle East, Africa and Asia.

When it undertakes a project outside Canada, SNC-LAVALIN Environment usually hires local specialists or enters into joint
venture agreements with local firms to ensure that its team, which is always headed by a senior Canadian professional with
international experience, benefits from a full understanding of the local context. This approach also minimizes the cost of its
services.

Services provided by SNC¨LAVALIN Environment Inc. :
· Air and Acoustics: air quality control, and noise and vibration control;
· Construction and Materials Quality Control: large works, structural steels, laboratory tests and analyses, inspection and supervision of construction, concrete and building materials;
· Contaminated Sites: site characterization and site restoration;
· Environmental Audits: transaction conformity and management audits including ISO 14001 auditing;
· Environmental Impact Assessments: including industrial projects, linear infrastructures, marine projects, resource management projects, and assistance in the acquisition of requisite permits;
· Fisheries and the Aquatic Environment: physical oceanography and marine geophysics, chemistry and biology of fresh and salt
water, sedimentology, coastal management, and fisheries;
· Geology and Geotechnical Studies: studies, laboratory tests and analyses, cold regions engineering, design, plans and
specifications and pile foundations;
· Groundwater: hydrogeological studies, and contaminant hydrogeology;
· Mining Sites: project impact assessments, site development plans, site closure plans, and mine waste disposal;
· Rural Development: classification of agricultural lands, natural resource management, master planning, irrigation and drainage systems, assistance to farmers and water users associations, management, training and agricultural extension;
· Solid and Hazardous Waste: professional services related to municipal, commercial, industrial, and hazardous wastes and the
design of handling facilities (landfills, incinerators, etc.);
· Training and Technical Assistance: training courses in environment, institutional strengthening, environment management
systems (ISO 14000), and environmental technical assistance in a variety of environmental disciplines; and
· Water Supply and Sanitation: water supply, water treatment, water distribution, waste water collection and treatment, and
management of storm water.

Product Name:
Environmental Products and Services
Product Name:
Air quality modelling, emission inventories, monitoring and stack sampling.
Emission inventories, whether obtained through stack sampling or estimates, provide a clear understanding of contaminant emissions from industrial facilities or power plants. In conjunction with advanced air quality modelling techniques, it is possible to estimate impacts related to these emissions. For proposed facilities, this technique can assist in predicting future impacts while helping to specify mitigation requirements. It also provides a cost-effective technique for assessing air pollution control requirements. Modelling and impact assessment are often required for regulatory approvals. For existing facilities, predictive techniques and impacts can be confirmed and quantified by stack sampling or ambient air monitoring. SNC-Lavalin Environment has the capabilities and expertise to undertake all aspects of air quality assessments. They have performed these assessments for numerous sectors including: pulp and paper mills, aluminum smelters, automotive parts manufacturing, chemical production facilities, electronic manufacturing, mining operations, sewage treatment plants, fossil fuel power plants, cogeneration facilities, landfills, municipal solid waste incinerators and, biomedical waste incinerators.

Product Name:
Full service project management.
Often site clean ups and remediation are complicated by the multi-disciplinary nature of these projects. Experience has proven that clean ups and decommissioning can be much more cost-effective and reliable, if executed by a full service company. The SNC-Lavalin team offers a full range of services from preliminary (Phase I) site investigations, through to complete site remediation either as EPCM or turnkey. While they do not own a specific remediation technology, they can provide solutions or recommendations using the most appropriate methodology for the site. SNC-Lavalin services include, RIFS, RAP, risk assessment, site management and monitoring.

Product Name:
Environmental assessment - multi-disciplinary.
Full environmental assessment provides detailed knowledge of social, environmental, risk and health impacts. Assessment provides detailed review of impacts, mitigation, and monitoring requirements and is often a requirement for project funding and regulatory approval. SNC-Lavalin provides an in-house multi-disciplinary team capable of managing all aspects of impact assessment including baseline studies, public consultation, regulatory negotiations, multi-media modelling and assessment, and post-construction monitoring in all media. Their experienced and diverse team offers cost-effective results. SNC-Lavalin has world-wide experience which has been applied to pulp and paper mills, landfills, waste management facilities, industrial plants, power generation, nuclear facilities, etc.

The mandate of SNC-Lavalin began in July 2012 and includes the following services:

 Preparation of the Project Notice pursuant to the Quebec Environment Quality Act (LEQ) and of the Project Description pursuant to the Canadian Environmental Assessment Act (2012) and meeting with key government authorities (MDDEFP, CEAA);
 Corridor analysis and pipeline route optimization in cooperation with the engineering team;
 Participation in information and public consultation activities including Aboriginal Communities;
 Preparation of the environmental and social impact assessment;
 Mapping and data integration in a GIS;
 Preparation of applications and support to obtain Certificates of compliance for the Project with municipal regulations (municipalities and MRCs) / certificates of authorization and other approvals from provincial authorities (MDDEFP, MRN);

Success stories:

North Shore Extension - Natural Gas Pipeline
Environmental and social impact assessment study and all related authorization requests under an Engineering, Procurement, and Construction Management project (EPCM) for Gaz Metro. The project was posponed by Gaz Metro after completion of preliminary engineering detailed cost estimate and environmental and social impact studies were submitted to provincial authorities.
The mandate of SNC-Lavalin began in July 2012 and includes the following services:

 Preparation of the Project Notice pursuant to the Quebec Environment Quality Act (LEQ) and of the Project Description pursuant to the Canadian Environmental Assessment Act (2012) and meeting with key government authorities (MDDEFP, CEAA);
 Corridor analysis and pipeline route optimization in cooperation with the engineering team;
 Participation in information and public consultation activities including Aboriginal Communities;
 Preparation of the environmental and social impact assessment;
 Mapping and data integration in a GIS;
 Preparation of applications and support to obtain Certificates of compliance for the Project with municipal regulations (municipalities and MRCs) / certificates of authorization and other approvals from provincial authorities (MDDEFP, MRN);
 Preparation and support to obtain the authorization from CPTAQ (Quebec Comission for Agriculture Protection Territory);
 Easement and land acquisitions: Acquisition and processing of data for each crossed property; Land, forestry and agricultural evaluations.
