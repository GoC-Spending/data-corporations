Company Profile


We're active members of the communities we serve. That's why at Stantec, we always design with community in mind.

Product Name:
Vapour extraction (also called vacuum extraction, soil venting, and in situ volatilization)
Vapour extraction is an effective technique for in situ remediation of sites contaminated with hydrocarbons or other volatile chemicals. The technology involves the application of a vacuum to the vadose zone to remove the soil gases, causing evaporation and removal of the volatile compounds. It is accepted by regulatory agencies and has been a highly successful treatment technology for vadose zone soils.

Two-phase vapour extraction, a variation on the basic technique, can remove soil gases, free product and contaminated groundwater. Its main advantages are achieving remedial objectives in situ within a relatively short time frame in a cost-effective manner. Also, in most cases, the technology can be applied using simple, reliable and readily available equipment with minimum site disruption.

Product Name:
Air dispersion modelling
Air dispersion modelling is the primary solution for determining atmospheric pollutant levels at points distant from a source, such as the impact of an industry on nearby residential areas. The modelling involves knowledge of the pollutant source, geography and climatological conditions. Models such as ISC2 for industrial sources like exhaust stacks and landfills or CALINE4 for traffic pollution are recognized by regulators as providing reasonably accurate predictions of pollutant concentrations.

These programs combined have the capability of providing city wide air quality models, including all industries and accounting for traffic patterns. With the correct information, air quality modelling can be performed anywhere, and relies mainly on the quality of the source information and computer capability.

Product Name:
Risk assessment
Risk assessment is an evaluation process used to describe the nature and magnitude of the risk associated with exposure of human or ecological receptors to a potential hazard (i.e. chemicals or potentially hazardous activities). Risk assessment typically involves: 1) problem formation (contaminant, pathway and receptor screening, development of a conceptual model of exposure); 2) data analysis (exposure and toxicity assessment); and 3) risk characterization (risk estimation, uncertainty analysis).

Risk assessment can be either qualitative or quantitative, depending on the nature and extent of the potential hazard. Risk management is the process of identification and implementation of appropriate measures to reduce or prevent potential risks. As part of the risk management process, effective risk communication is essential to ensure that all affected individuals understand the nature of the potential risk, the possible effects and the importance of correct implementation of any risk management strategy.

Product Name:
In situ bioremediation
In situ bioremediation involves the use of bacteria to naturally degrade dissolved hydrocarbon compounds in groundwater. The process requires creating the necessary environmental conditions to promote the growth of hydrocarbon degrading bacteria. These bacteria can be either naturally occurring or added to the groundwater. The primary environmental factor which influences biodegradation is the availability of oxygen to promote microbial growth. In addition, nutrients can be added to further promote microbial growth where necessary.

Oxygen can be delivered to the groundwater through a number of processes including:
(1) injecting air into the groundwater (air sparging);

(2) re-injection of groundwater which has been aerated on surface; and

(3) injection of hydrogen peroxide into the groundwater.

The particular method used depends upon a number of factors, including soil and contaminant characteristics. In situ bioremediation is an emerging technology which has successfully been applied to remediation of dissolved hydrocarbon contaminants under a variety of site conditions.
