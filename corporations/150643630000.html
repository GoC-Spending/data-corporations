Company Profile


Since 1967, Markland Specialty Engineering Limited has been helping Municipal and Industrial Plants monitor and automate control of water and wastewater treatment, and process slurries.

Markland products are used in clarifiers and lagoons (to monitor the interface level of primary, secondary, return-activated and backwash sludge) and in pipelines and tanks (to monitor process slurries).

The Sludge Gun® is a handheld, portable Sludge Blanket Level Detector, used to spot-check the interface level of solids in liquids.

Automatic Sludge Blanket Level Detectors allow users to program sludge removal pumps to operate only when necessary, allowing sludge density to be optimized and preventing carryover in the de-watering process.

Suspended Solids Density Meters monitor slurry and sludge concentrations, helping users optimize polymer/chemical dosing, automate de-sludge pumps, and achieve preferred concentrations before further processing.

Automatic Duckbill Sampling Systems help municipal and industrial plants automate composite sampling of tanks, sumps, open channels and sewers. They allow users to monitor process slurries and effluent, and facilitate monitoring for environmental regulatory compliance. They are inherently explosion-proof, and able to move samples up high lifts (more than 24m) and over long runs (over 30m), even in freezing temperatures.

Markland designs and manufactures instruments that improve process efficiency and effectiveness in municipal water/wastewater facilities, and industries as diverse as food, mineral, chemical, pharmaceutical, and petrochemical processing, pulp & paper manufacturing, power generation, and mining.

Markland provides, at no-charge, a complete engineering report of its instruments' response to customers' material. Markland products meet the needs of unique applications. Delivery is quick and support is factory-direct.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Suspended Solids Density Meters
Suspended Solids Density Meters, from Markland Specialty Engineering, help municipal and industrial plants monitor slurry and sludge concentrations in tanks and pipelines, and automate sludge removal.

This instrument uses non-radioactive ultrasound to monitor the concentration of process slurries and wastewater sludge in tanks, pipelines and clarifiers. It measures mix tank inflow densities, allowing operators to adjust fluid or particulate amounts, and polymer/chemical dosing, for preferred concentrations: to maximize settling while minimizing flocculent dosage. It allows users to program underflow pumps for optimum density for feeding to belt presses and centrifuges, and to improve outflow available for reuse.

The Meter uses a throw-in style probe or inline spool-piece. Its non-intrusive sensor acts as both transmitter and receiver of the inherently safe ultrasound waves. The output signal is continuously sent to a computer or control system, providing a linear reading of the percentage of suspended solids, and enabling calculation of Solids Mass Flow. Readings are not affected by the colour of particulates or fluid. It measures process slurries, and primary, secondary and return-activated sludge, even if concentrations are too thick for optical methods. Observing changes in density may give operators early notice of breaks in filtration material. This device is simple to set-up and calibrate.


Product Name:
Automatic Sludge Blanket Level Detectors
Automatic Sludge Blanket Level Detectors, from Markland Specialty Engineering Ltd., help municipal and industrial plants monitor and control sludge levels in waste water clarifiers and other tanks and automate sludge removal.

This Detector uses high-intensity infrared light beams to help monitor interface levels in clarifiers (including lamellas, dissolved air flotation thickeners, decanting tanks and sequencing batch reactors), when clarifying incoming process water or wastewater. It measures primary, secondary and return-activated sludge, as well as backwash sludge from sand/membrane filters.

The Detector allows users to program sludge removal pumps to operate only when necessary: preventing carryover; avoiding pumping thin sludge or large volumes of water (when a core hole is pulled); optimizing sludge density before subsequent processing (such as feeding to belt presses, digesters and centrifuges), and improving the quality and quantity of water available for reuse. It provides data that may indicate inefficient polymer/chemical dosing. In addition, features are uniquely suited for specific applications. For example, in DAF units, the detector can adjust the speed of the surface skimmer to match the varying thicknesses of the floating sludge layer; in SBRs, it can control the decant valve, enabling cycle time to be kept to a minimum. Eliminating unnecessary pumping reduces wear-and-tear on pumps, downtime for maintenance, and energy usage.
The detector (4- and 8-foot models are available) uses a vertical probe submerged in the tank. Sixty-four LEDs are placed within a gap running along the length of the probe. Each shines across this gap to a matching phototransistor sensor. These pairs are sequentially scanned to locate the sludge bed. Beam power intensity automatically adjusts to accommodate different sludge and overlying liquid concentrations. The slim profile, with lights and sensors contained within the probe gap, make it well suited for obstructed or constricted areas; for example, readings are not affected by the curved walls of lamellas. The probe is placed at a height that enables the operator to maintain a desired sludge bed depth. When the sludge level reaches the preferred maximum height, the detector sends a signal to the control system or computer to trigger the desludge pump. Once the ideal minimum sludge level is met, an output signal turns the pumps off. The all-PVC probe is corrosion-free. Analog and relay outputs are available. No calibration is required with this simple-to-install detector. A wireless data link is available as an optional feature or upgrade.
Reducing energy usage, without sacrificing process effectiveness and/or production quality, results in benefits that extend well beyond the specific municipal or industrial application to benefit the broader environmental setting.


Product Name:
Automatic Duckbill Sampling Systems
Automatic Duckbill Sampling Systems, from Markland Specialty Engineering, automate the collection of composite samples from tanks and open channels to monitor process slurries and effluent.

This unique system uses compressed air and the rubber Duckbill, acting as a check valve, to collect samples from tanks, sumps and sewers, and allows users to program the controller to sample based on time or by a flow meter, sample multiple sites simultaneously, and facilitate the monitoring required for environmental regulatory compliance.

It is able to move samples up high lifts (more than 24m/79ft vertically) and over long runs (more than 30m/98ft horizontally), even in freezing temperatures. The control box for the compressed air can be located far from the sampling site and requires only an air-line to run to the sampler head. Liquid flows into the submerged sampler head through a bottom inlet (drawn by hydrostatic pressure in the vented hoses), up through the rubber Duckbill and into the hoses, until it is equal to the level of the outer tank or sump. The body of the sampler fills only as high as the top fitting for the vented airlines, trapping a pocket of air in its dome. The sampler is now ready for a burst of compressed air, which increases the pressure inside the sampler head, squeezing the rubber Duckbill and causing it to close and expel any large particles back through the bottom inlet. Simultaneously, this forces the complete sample through the lower fitting and up the hose into the collection bottle.

This system is inherently explosion-proof (no pump; no vacuum system). Lines are blown clear and dry after each sample. Users may call in manual samples at any time without affecting the normal sampling interval. Modular design means components, including small compressors, can be selected and assembled to suit the customers sampling requirements. The sampler head is available in aluminium, stainless steel, and PVC, to accommodate specific pH levels.


Product Name:
Portable Sludge Blanket Level Detectors - The Sludge Gun®
Portable Sludge Blanket Level Detectors, from Markland Specialty Engineering, help sewage treatment plants monitor sludge levels in clarifiers, tanks and lagoons.

This instrument uses a high-intensity infrared light beam to monitor blanket interface levels in clarifiers, settlement tanks, lagoons and ponds. It allows users to eliminate unnecessary pumping, and to optimize sludge density before subsequent processing.

A trigger switch is located on the handle of the Sludge Gun®. When depressed, it causes a beam of high-intensity infrared light to shine across a gap in the probe to a matching phototransistor sensor. When the LED beam encounters suspended particles in the probe gap, some of its energy is absorbed, causing the phototransistor to activate a rear-facing speaker on the butt of the gun. As the percentage of suspended solids increases, the audible tone changes in both pitch and volume.

The Sludge Gun® probe is lowered into the tank or pond. By slowly moving the probe up and down, the operator can observe the numbered depth markers on the cable and establish the location of both the true settled bed as well as the overlying unsettled cloud layer. A thumb-adjustable sensitivity control allows the user to detect suspended solids concentrations ranging from light flocs to thick blankets. In addition, model features are uniquely suited for specific applications. For example, high-power Model 10-HP works well in murky lagoons; Model 10-LP detects clear liquid surfaces. No calibration is required.

The spring-loaded trigger turns off the gun, preventing accidental drain on the battery. The cable is stored on a spool at the front of the gun. Battery life (standard AA) averages one year with normal use. This instrument is compact, rugged and weatherproof, making it ideal for use from narrow catwalks and boats.
