Company Profile


Zeidler Partnership Architects is a Canadian architectural firm focused on design excellence, innovation and working with clients in a range of complex project types. One of the longest continually running practices in North America, we are based in Toronto, Ontario, and we are an international firm with offices in Calgary (Zeidler BKDI), Victoria, London, Berlin, Beijing, Chengdu, and Abu Dhabi. Senior partner Vaidila Banelis, and partners Rob Eley, Jurgen Henze, Andrea Richardson, and Ping Zhang, lead a dedicated team whose architectural vision is informed by sound practical expertise.

Our ability to create engaging and inspiring buildings has been recognized by more than 160 awards, including eight Governor General’s Medals, Canada’s highest architectural honour, and five legacy Landmark Awards from the Ontario Association of Architects. We celebrate these achievements with our clients and we share with them the ongoing stories as these buildings stand the test of time.

Quality is the foundation of our practice — quality of design, process, and service. We believe that excellent architecture emerges from a project’s physical and social relationships with its surroundings, and from a client’s unique culture. Our goal is to fulfill each client’s functional and economic requirements while evoking a positive response from the people who will use the space. The result is buildings that are a pleasure to own and to be in — buildings that are at once enduring, relevant, and inventive, buildings that contribute to their communities and are catalysts for new ways of thinking.

Service Name:
Architect - Health Care and Life Science Design and Planning
Zeidler Partnership Architects is a recognized leader in this specialized area of design and has served more than 40 health care and life science clients. Projects include large and small complexes where the scope of work ranges from major new hospitals to renovations and additions. This intensive experience has allowed us to develop a number of innovative systems and techniques that can significantly reduce the cost of delivering medical care.
As health care is remarkably complex, the buildings we have designed meet highly specialized needs. Our most noted projects include the McMaster University Health Sciences Centre (Hamilton) and Walter C. Mackenzie Health Sciences Centre (Edmonton). Toronto area facilities include The Hospital for Sick Children Atrium Patient Tower, Ontario Cancer Institute/Princess Margaret Hospital, and Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centres Clinical Services Wing. Other projects include the University of Maryland Medical Systems Homer Gudelsky Building in Baltimore, Coswig Heart Centre (Herzzentrum) in Germany and Detroit Receiving Hospital and Wayne State University Health Care Institute.
Our work also includes such renewal projects as the Humber River Regional Hospital (Additions & Renovations), Ottawa Civic Hospital, Mississauga Hospital and St. Pauls Hospital (Grey Nuns) in Saskatoon. In addition, we have designed a number of smaller community hospitals for Ajax, Whitby and Sussex; a series of additions and alterations to the Scarborough General Hospital, Izaak Walton Hospital (Halifax), Ross Memorial Hospital (Lindsay), Guelph General Hospital, and Brantford General Hospital; many laundries and service buildings such as those for the University Hospital in Edmonton; and medical offices and laboratories in the Montréal Childrens Hospital.

Technology profile

The firm's extensive computer resources include a CAD system with in-house plotters that permit plotting at any time. CAD drawings are routinely exchanged for electronic coordination of projects throughout all phases of development. Communication between all ZRP/A offices is fully networked by means of the Internet and modem, an FTP site and an electronic Bulletin Board System. Remote network access provides staff members with mobile notebook connectivity. These facilities provide the firm with complete communication between consultants, clients and project site offices.

Zeidler is the leading Canadian architectural practice in Building Information Modelling having commenced 3 dimensional modelling for design and technical documents since 2002.

With our extensive experience, gleaned from the integrated construction documentation on Canadas largest construction project at the BOW EnCana headquarters in Calgary, as well as other major projects, we are now able to employ all of the data extraction and coordination capabilities available in AutoDesks Revit Software.
