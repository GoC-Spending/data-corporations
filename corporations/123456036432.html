Company Profile


SCIEX was incorporated in 1974 by a group from the University of Toronto Institute of Aerospace Studies. In 1981 SCIEX was acquired by MDS Health Group Limited.
The key to SCIEX's success has been the innovative approach that it has taken to the development of instrumentation. In every product line, SCIEX has been the pioneer with the introduction of the first commercial mobile laboratories, triple quadruple mass spectrometers, inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometers, contraband detection systems, and electrospray based mass spectrometers. Many of these products are in the second and third generation stages.
The development of innovative products which meet the needs of niche markets have been critical to company's success. SCIEX has twice been awarded the Canada Award for Business Excellence, once in the category of spectrometry and secondly in the category of Innovation for the development of the API III system. SCIEX frequently works in partnership with our clients to develop new solutions to client problems. Joint venture partnerships have been a key component of the strategy to market products world wide, notably with Applied Biosystems Inc. and PerkinElmer Inc.

Product Name:
Mobile tandem mass spectrometer analytical laboratory
The mobile laboratory transports a tandem mass spectrometer system and other analytical instruments directly to the location of a pollution problem. The system is capable of analyzing toxic air pollutants, volatile organic compounds and odorous compounds in real time at concentrations of a few parts-per-trillion in both air and aqueous media.

The laboratory is supplied to companies or agencies as a turnkey operation for use as an emergency response tool and to track plumes, to locate airborne fugitive emissions and to allow fence line monitoring of contaminated sites. Waterborne polluted samples can be taken directly to the on-site laboratory and monitored immediately.
