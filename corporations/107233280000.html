Company Profile


Tetra Tech, headquartered in Pasadena, California, is a leading provider of consulting, engineering, program management, construction, procurement and technical services addressing the resource management and infrastructure markets. Tetra Tech supports government and commercial clients through innovative solutions focused on water, the environment, and energy. With 13,000 employees and 380 offices worldwide, Tetra Techs capabilities span the entire project life cycle.

During the past two years, Tetra Tech has grown into one of the largest engineering firms in Canada. With more than 3,900 employees across the country, Tetra Tech is a diverse and full-service engineering firm. Tetra Techs Canadian roots are traced to the following well-respected firms: Wardrop (now known as Tetra Tech WEI Inc.), BPR, EBA Engineering Consultants Ltd. (EBA), and Fransen Engineering Ltd. (Fransen).

Today, Canada represents one-third of Tetra Techs worldwide employee baseand is still growing.

Tetra Tech provides comprehensive and integrated licensing, environmental, engineering design, facilities construction, and construction support for a full suite of services related to power generation, transmission and distribution, hydropower, and dam projects worldwide. Total company revenues at the completion of the last fiscal year (2011) were in excess of $2.7B.

Service Name:
CONSULTING ENGINEER - ENERGY - NUCLEAR
Much has been said about the nuclear renaissance and the need for a sustainable energy source. Nuclear energy emits no greenhouse gases and produces base load energy to support industries and residential communities.

For more than 40 years, Wardrop has supported nuclear energy by providing multi-disciplined engineering services to operators of nuclear generating facilities for the safe and efficient operation of nuclear power stations and the responsible management of nuclear by-products.

Throughout our history, Wardrop has developed an in-depth understanding of the nuclear industry, our clients needs and solid relations with the Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission. This knowledge and experience allows us to create targeted solutions and expedite their implementation.

DESIGN ENGINEERING:
The Wardrop team is comprised of technical specialists and professionals representing all the various disciplines. By pulling upon the diverse skill sets, we are able to create tailored solutions specific to each client and each project.

Plant Upgrades and Retrofits: Wardrop generates multi-disciplinary design engineering designs that are executed in accordance with nuclear engineering change control (ECC) and CSA N286 quality requirements.

Waste Management: We have experience in developing solutions for safe and efficient handling of solid and liquid waste, as well as the design of enabling facilities for medium to long term storage.

Control Software: Our team offers complete digital control computer (DCC) software support required for replacement and upgrade of components and control systems at nuclear sites.

Nuclear Facilities: Wardrop has delivered on new facilities and the enhancement of ancillary service buildings at various nuclear facilities.

Our continuing success is a result of the specialized expertise and our inherent ability to approach every challenge from a creative point of view. Creativity breeds innovation and Wardrop is renowned for delivering innovative, cost-effective solutionson-time and on budgetto some of the biggest names in the nuclear industry.

PROJECT & CONSTRUCTION MANAGEMENT:
Wardrop is fully engaged with clients to understand their short- and long-term goals, and business processes. This gives our client that much more confidence when our professional team provides overall project management during engineering, procurement, and construction phases of capital projects.

Work Planning: We work with our clients to develop comprehensive work plans and contractor work scopes for modifications installations within the nuclear site.

Materials Management: Our team supports vendor interface and materials management through Enterprise Work Management databases.

Contract Monitoring: Wardrop staff provide hands-on contract monitoring of trade contractors to ensure progress, milestone adherence and design conformance.

Field Engineering: Field Changes and Technical Support during installation to ensure safe operation while progressing modifications.

Commissioning: To ensure a smooth transition to operations, we develop commissioning specifications and test protocols. All of this is captured in thorough reports for future reference.

ASSET MANAGEMENT
Nuclear generating facilities have a planned life span of 40 years or more. It is the planned maintenance and replacement of the components and systems that permits optimum operations and high capacity factors. Wardrop possesses substantial experience in the development and execution of various asset management programs including maintenance optimization, condition assessments, and equipment qualification for nuclear and conventional systems. We employ industry leading processes and tools to optimize asset utilization.

Master Equipment List: Our team works with the client to develop a Master Equipment list (MEL), Operational Flow Sheets and nuclear safety criticality coding of equipment. This identifies safety related components and can be enrolled in a maintenance optimization program.

Preventative Maintenance Optimization (PMO): We apply industry best practices for maintenance programs that align with Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI) and Institute of Nuclear Power Operations (INPO) guidelines.

Work Management: Wardrop is diligent in our work assessment and management so that field execution is conducted in a safe and timely manner. Extra attention is given to ensuring procedures, tools, and materials are current and available for use.

Condition Assessment: We have experience in providing System and Component Level Condition Assessments (CCAs) to establish ageing mechanisms, failure modes, and critical maintenance requirements.

The James A. Richardson International Airport (YWG) in Winnipeg, Manitoba embarked on a $585 M Airport Site Redevelopment (ASR) program in 2004. The project balances the Winnipeg Airport Authoritys (WAA) vision of leading transportation innovation, while reflecting its prairie roots and heritage. The complex ASR project entails 51,000 m² signature terminal building, new, four-level car parking garage and elevated roadway, aircraft apron expansion, drainage, and power house upgrades. To advocate on their behalf, the WAA engaged Wardrop Engineering Inc. to act as the Owners Engineer. Acting as the owners advocate, Wardrop manages those contracts fairly and legally. Beyond contract management, the owners engineer coordinates between the owner, designers and contractors so that everyone understands each other and maintains a balance of design integrity, cost savings and construction schedule. With the onset of construction, Wardrop also monitored the Contractors Quality Control program.

Expansion and Upgrades at City of Thunder Bays Water Pollution Control Plant

The Great Lakes is the largest fresh water resource in the world. Maintaining the integrity of Lake Superior, the most northern of the great lakes, is a priority for the City of Thunder Bay. The Citys commitment was first demonstrated through the development of a Pollution Prevention and Control Plant in 1964 which was designed by Wardrop. This commitment was further enacted through the expansion and addition of secondary treatment at the Citys Atlantic Avenue Water Pollution Control Plant (WPCP). Wardrop partnered with Reid Crowther to design and construct this $75M major expansion and retrofit of the existing Atlantic Avenue Water Pollution Control Plant, creating a new state-of-the-art 265 ML/day Secondary Sewage Treatment Plant. The design was initiated in January 2000, and the project was commissioned fall 2005. Wardrop was responsible for the detailed design of the civil, structural, utility mechanical, and electrical.

Pickering B NGS Condition Assessment

The Pickering Nuclear Generating Station (NGS) consists of eight CANDU nuclear reactors and is divided into Pickering A (4 units) and Pickering B (4 units). The first Pickering B unit went in-service on May 10, 1983 and the last unit went in-service on February 28, 1986. The original design life for these units is 40 years. OPG (owner/operator) initiated a Refurbishment Study to determine the feasibility of extending Pickering B operational life to 2050-2060. Wardrop performed a Condition Assessment of the Pickering B NGS systems, structures, and components (SSCs), as well as outbuildings (70), as part of the Plant Life Extension Project. Wardrop performed more than 350 individual component* condition assessments and finalized 65 System Condition Assessment (SCA) reports that summarized the ageing management evaluation for each system and its components. Specific recommendations addressed requirements to achieve safe and reliable operation for the stations planned end-of-life, as well as additional recommendations for life extension.

Service Name:
CONSULTING ENGINEER - GREEN/BROWNFIELD DEVELOPMENT
Brownfields are former industrial or commercial developments that no longer operate and stand empty. Many are contaminated (leaking underground fuel storage tanks, on-site disposal of waste materials or dumping of process by-products) and many are abandoned. This creates liability uncertainty, which devalues the property and increases the risk for potential investors. Wardrop has the technical resources to complete a thorough assessment of the property, identify and evaluate the risks, and develop and manage a comprehensive remediation plan to bring the land up to standards suitable for reuse.

Typical services performed by Wardrop in this regard include:
- Identification and delineation of contaminated areas
- Identification of human and environmental health risks around these areas
- Development of risk mitigation strategies, including cost estimating
- Liaison with regulatory authorities
- Long-term environmental monitoring

James A. Richardson International Airport (YWG)

The James A. Richardson International Airport (YWG) in Winnipeg, Manitoba embarked on a $585 M Airport Site Redevelopment (ASR) program in 2004. The project balances the Winnipeg Airport Authoritys (WAA) vision of leading transportation innovation, while reflecting its prairie roots and heritage. The complex ASR project entails 51,000 m² signature terminal building, new, four-level car parking garage and elevated roadway, aircraft apron expansion, drainage, and power house upgrades. To advocate on their behalf, the WAA engaged Wardrop Engineering Inc. to act as the Owners Engineer. Acting as the owners advocate, Wardrop manages those contracts fairly and legally. Beyond contract management, the owners engineer coordinates between the owner, designers and contractors so that everyone understands each other and maintains a balance of design integrity, cost savings and construction schedule. With the onset of construction, Wardrop also monitored the Contractors Quality Control program.

Expansion and Upgrades at City of Thunder Bays Water Pollution Control Plant

The Great Lakes is the largest fresh water resource in the world. Maintaining the integrity of Lake Superior, the most northern of the great lakes, is a priority for the City of Thunder Bay. The Citys commitment was first demonstrated through the development of a Pollution Prevention and Control Plant in 1964 which was designed by Wardrop. This commitment was further enacted through the expansion and addition of secondary treatment at the Citys Atlantic Avenue Water Pollution Control Plant (WPCP). Wardrop partnered with Reid Crowther to design and construct this $75M major expansion and retrofit of the existing Atlantic Avenue Water Pollution Control Plant, creating a new state-of-the-art 265 ML/day Secondary Sewage Treatment Plant. The design was initiated in January 2000, and the project was commissioned fall 2005. Wardrop was responsible for the detailed design of the civil, structural, utility mechanical, and electrical.

Pickering B NGS Condition Assessment

The Pickering Nuclear Generating Station (NGS) consists of eight CANDU nuclear reactors and is divided into Pickering A (4 units) and Pickering B (4 units). The first Pickering B unit went in-service on May 10, 1983 and the last unit went in-service on February 28, 1986. The original design life for these units is 40 years. OPG (owner/operator) initiated a Refurbishment Study to determine the feasibility of extending Pickering B operational life to 2050-2060. Wardrop performed a Condition Assessment of the Pickering B NGS systems, structures, and components (SSCs), as well as outbuildings (70), as part of the Plant Life Extension Project. Wardrop performed more than 350 individual component* condition assessments and finalized 65 System Condition Assessment (SCA) reports that summarized the ageing management evaluation for each system and its components. Specific recommendations addressed requirements to achieve safe and reliable operation for the stations planned end-of-life, as well as additional recommendations for life extension.
