Company Profile


SEI has cultivated a unique capability not readily found elsewhere in the world by designing, engineering and manufacturing products from innovative industrial fabrics. These fabrics solve specific problems for customers and provide advantages not available with products made from traditional rigid materials.

SEI was incorporated in May, 1978, and, since 1982, the company has focused its expertise on creating solutions most often used by the military and by exploration, utility, industrial, decontamination and humanitarian industries. SEI has a global agent network with two international offices and seven international repair facilities. SEI products are in use in 110 countries around the world.

In 2016, SEI Industries is the first manufacturer to offer shop-manufactured secondary containment products that meet the CAN/ULC-S668-12 requirement and are certified by Intertek. SEI also became the first to produce fuel storage bladders certified to the CAN/CSA-B837-14 - Collapsible Fabric Storage Tanks standard and meets Environment Canada Storage Tank Systems for Petroleum Products and Allied Petroleum Products Regulations (SOR/2008-197).

Product Name:
Arctic King Fuel Tank
The Arctic King collapsible fuel tank bladder is designed to store fuels in arctic environments. It can be deployed in temperatures as low as -50º C and withstand high levels of ultra-violet (UV). This tank meets Environment Canadas Technical Requirements for Collapsible Fabric Storage Tanks (Bladders) published in 2009. Arctic King tanks are lightweight and fully collapsible, offering liquid containment many times larger than their transportable size. Arctic King tanks are made from proprietary materials and are 100% radio frequency welded, seam sealed and pressure tested. Sizes range from 500 USG to 30,000 USG (2000-113,000 L). Arctic King tanks are constructed from Arctic King fabric which was purpose-engineered by SEI for above-ground storage of fuels in arctic climates at remote sites. Arctic King fabric has a low cold crack temp below -50F or -46C, meets the CAN/CSA B-837-14 standard for collapsible fabric storage tanks (bladders). It was developed to withstand long duration fuel exposure. Exclusive Arctic King fabric exceeds all US military specifications and provides a number of advantages that ultimately help to preserve fuel quality and extend equipment life and safety.

Product Name:
Desert King Fuel Tank
The Jungle King collapsible fuel tank bladder is designed for storage of liquid fuels in tropical climates with high temperatures, high levels of ultra-violet (UV) and high humidity. The tanks are lightweight and fully collapsible, offering liquid containment many times larger than their transportable size. Jungle King tanks are made from proprietary materials and are 100% radio frequency welded, seams sealed and pressure tested. Sizes range from 500 USG to 30,000 USG (2000-113,000 L). Desert King tanks are constructed from a fabric that was purposely engineered by SEI for above-ground storage of fuels in desert climates at remote sites. Certified Desert King fabric is double-offset coated to provide the lowest diffusion rates in the industry and meets the CAN/CSA-B837-14 standard for collapsible fabric storage tanks (bladders). This is an important aspect in desert climates where temperatures can cause excess vapour accumulation. Double-offset coating significantly slows this damaging condition and lengthens service life.

Unique applications:

Over the past three decades, SEI has been fortunate to support many high-profile projects including:

1. Siberias Bema Gold: During winter conditions, SEI delivered, installed and connected a huge two million gallon diesel fuel tank farm for exploration activities in one of the worlds largest gold deposits.

2. Perus Repsol: Supplied 26 Jungle King fuel tanks, featuring a new proprietary fabric that is far more resistant to humidity, temperature and sunlight than any other tank on the market.

3. Taiwans National Fire Agency: Assisted in reducing fire response times from 24 hours to less than four hours through the delivery of a multi-faceted collection of equipment and services.

4. Yukons Selwyn Resources: At the top of two raw mountain sites, SEI provided a complete turnkey 400,000 litre fuel system that allowed the establishment of two airstrips and base camps for mining exploration.

5. Canadas Armed Forces: As a long-term defence supplier, SEI created a new high-pressure aviation refuelling system that is in service around the world.

Success stories:

SEI is proud to be a manufacturer of many products that assist with humanitarian efforts, help protect the environment as well as support commercial and military operations in 110 countries around the world. Often, these products are seen in the news without many realizing that the product was born and made in Canada.

As an example, the list below provides a small sampling of the type of global roles that SEI products have played:

1. Nuclear site cool-down after Japans devastating tsunami (using the Bambi Bucket)
2. Clean, potable water storage during Haitis earthquake relief effort (using the Onion tank)
3. Assistance with Columbias drug enforcement activities (using the BATT and Jungle King tank)
4. Custom decontamination trailers for use in biological, radiological and terrorist events (FBI, FEMA, military hospitals and the National Guard)
5. Critical firefighting products that are used from Australia to Italy to Asia (80% of the worlds market share)
6. Robust remote site solutions that are designed for specific climates and help to create new jobs from resource exploration, construction and development activities in countries around the world (e.g. mining and drilling operations)
7. Military solutions that minimize risks during combat and support civilian emergency response (fuel storage, fuel pump systems, water storage)
8. Secondary containment products that protect the environment, prevent spills and ensure compliance to regulations (SEI products are being used in the WWII DEW-Line clean-up effort)
