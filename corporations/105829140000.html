Company Profile


Richlu Manufacturing, founded in 1939 (as Richlu Sportswear), is a Canadian company specializing in the manufacture of quality workwear, safety and insulated outerwear. Our products are sold from coast to coast by department, chain, and independent retailers, and industrial distributors. Our brand labels include Tough Duck, Work King, Arctic King, Work Horse, Trailways, Golden Goose, Iceman and Natural Habitat. We also produce many private brand labels for major retailers in North America.

A major portion of our production is devoted to workwear, due in large part to the national success of our Tough Duck and Work
King product range. This success has enabled us to become the largest manufacturer of insulated work wear in Canada. Tough
Duck and Work King garments are prominently featured in many mail order catalogues and flyers throughout the country and these
brands are well known and respected in the industry. We also manufacture a range of street wear including jackets and parkas
featuring interlining in down, polyester and Thinsulate(tm).

All garments are produced in our own quality controlled factories in Manitoba and in southeast Asia. Our Winnipeg operation, serving as the company headquarters, is where the majority of domestic production is manufactured. We also maintain distribution centres in Winnipeg where finished product is warehoused for designated orders, as well as for quick response delivery to our customers.

Our product is also exported to the United States, Japan, New Zealand and Europe.

We take pride in marketing our product as tough, durable, warm, comfortable and completely affordable.

Hi,
Last weekend I was helping a friend do some fence clearing with a chainsaw and I was wearing my ToughDuck work pants. I had a bad accident and cut through my thigh pretty bad. Although the injury was severe I was assured when the doctors looked at what I was wearing I was lucky. They said if I were not wearing heavy work pants I would have cut right through my quad muscle instead of only partially cutting it.
Thanks for making great work wear. I am really glad I was wearing it at the time!
Scott

Hello –
I just wanted to tell you a story of what happened to my husband the night of August 1, 2012. My husband was riding his 2009 Harley Davidson Electric Glide home from work at 11:00 pm. He works for CPRail, so he was wearing his Tough Duck coveralls, which are his regular work clothes. He also had on a fairly heavy North Face jacket, and of course his work boots and motorcycle gloves.

About 2 km from the outskirts of Cranbrook, he hit a deer that ran across the highway in from of him. The deer ended up in the right hand ditch, which was the lane my husband was traveling in. My husband then lost control of his morotcycle, and they both skidded down the highway, across the oncoming lane.. My husband ended up on the highway, but the bike continued to slide and wound up going down a 10’ enbankment on the opposite side of the impact. He had been going 90 km and hour!

Our beloved bike is a write off due to the frame damage. My husband was extremely fortunate to walk away with scrapes and bruises. His jacket has holes and damage where he first hit the pavement on his right side, back and the majority of his sliding was on his left side. His main road rash is on his right knee and left hip. His thermos was in his backpack, and even that has damage from being scraped along the pavement. His helmet is damaged on the left side where he slid. His motorcycle gloves have holes in them, and you can see how the kevlar knuckle guards were ground down from his slide.

Attached are a couple of photos of his “Tough Duck” coveralls. You seriously, won’t believe this... There was a small hole on his right knee, but not through both layers. He had extensive “road rash” on that knee, layers deep! The left side of his coveralls has practically no damage, just a couple of holes, a ripped back pocket and wearing from his slide. He had a large area of “road rash” on his hip and thigh.

He had always purchased Carhart coveralls, as that was the only brand available at the two stores here in Cranbrook that sell work clothes. Earlier this year, we went to a UFA store in Lethbridge, AB. He thought he would give your product a try since the Carharts were always failing on the front at the knee area where the double stitching is. Needless to say, he loves your coveralls!! Our friend was in Alberta this past weekend, and we had him buy us another pair. Your product certainly lives up to it’s name.

Sincerely,
Fred and Mary
