Resumes and Career Change Consultation
Matt Stepchuk has been researching the subject of career transition and producing resumes since he took delivery of his first XT computer at age 18. In 1999, with the successful career coaching and packaging of more than 100 individuals under his belt, Matt was invited to design and deliver a one day "Job Hunter's Workshop" to three consecutive graduating classes at Algonquin Technology Institute. The workshops were a resounding success and entrenched Matt's position as a solid resource in this field.

This "Packaging People" section of www.infomatt.com is dedicated to sharing basic concepts and "tried and true" resources that Matt has recommended a thousand times and drawn upon in his advice to others.

To start, there are basically two types of job hunt  the traditional (fire your resume into the world like buckshot from a shotgun and hope for the best) and the systematic  figure out what you are meant to do, formalize your network, perform industry analysis, use your network to get to the key decision maker, "ace" the interview, negotiate effectively and build your empire.

The latter may sound overly complex but it isn't. There has never been a more important time to lay the groundwork to produce a quality result (I don't know how many times I've heard "I got the job" only to hear 6 months later "I hate this job"). Besides, there are many established tools and resources out there that make the groundwork easy and help you convert your nervous energy into structured results.

The handouts in the "Packaging People" these basic concepts in more detail. The three resources most often recommended by Matt are also included on the right. Clicking on them will bring you to Chapter's Bookstore where they can be ordered on-line. They are absolute dynamite.
