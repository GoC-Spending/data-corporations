Company Profile


Natural resource consulting firm providing a range of services to forestry, mining, energy, utility, transportation and public sectors, and First Nations resource management planning.

KBM has been serving public agencies, individuals, corporations, First Nations and non-governmental organizations across North America and internationally since it was incorporated in 1974.

KBM is a leading service provider in forest management planning, which requires a deep understanding of the economics of natural resources extraction in a manner that is socially acceptable and ensures the ecological integrity of the affected region. KBM also delivers an integrated range of consulting, field and technical services, including state of the art aerial photography and geomatics, to mining, energy and other resource sectors.

The company has three divisions: Consulting and Technical Services, Aerial Survey & Geomatics, and Sales and Service. The KBM Sales and Retail Store sells comprehensive lines of quality equipment and supplies to foresters, geologists, surveyors, tree planters, and other outdoor professionals.

Based in Thunder Bay, Ontario, Toronto and Prince Albert, KBM has 30 full-time employees, many with advanced degrees in natural resource management sciences. KBM also draws upon a national network of professionals and academics to complement the companys core competencies to deliver complex projects in natural resource inventory, planning, policy review and evaluation.

KBM supports the Canadian Institute of Forestry, Ontario Forest Industries Association, Ontario Professional Foresters Association, Society of American Foresters, Forest Ecosystem Science Cooperative, Ontario Prospector's Association, Iron Mining Association of Minnesota, Ontario Waterpower Association, Northwestern Ontario Air Carriers Association, and Thunder Bay Chamber of Commerce.

Success stories:

KBM Resources Group won the 2013 RBC Innovation Award for Innovative Company of the Year through the Northwestern Ontario Innovation Centre. The award recognizes KBMs leadership through the innovation of its products and services, the expansion of its business, and its dedication to community involvement.

One of KBMs practical innovations is the application of Entac, an environmentally friendly emulsion that can be used to finish trails and roads and seal stockpiles and tailing ponds. This by-product of the pulp and paper industry is used on sections of trail at Prince Arthurs Landing and Centennial Park in the City of Thunder Bay.

Collaboration makes KBMs innovation efforts successful, Van Damme explained. The Northwestern Ontario Innovation Centre facilitated our partnership with EnsSolutions, the creators of Entac, which makes this award particularly meaningful. The City of Thunder Bay was also instrumental in pioneering the first trials of Entac. Lakehead University, the Industrial Research Assistance Program, and others also share in our achievement.

KBM is an early adopter of leading edge technologies, and is often sought out by manufacturers to trial new equipment. In addition, KBM remains dynamic and open to new innovative solutions that can help its clients succeed in ways they have not previously considered.
