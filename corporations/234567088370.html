Company Profile


Scimega Research was founded to meet the industrys demand for superior clinical trial management services in the complex field of oncology clinical research. Our mission is to attract cutting-edge oncology compounds and clinical trials to Canada, and to connect drug developers with a network of highly motivated investigators and their patients.

The Reverse Feasibility Program is a collaborative network of Canada's top performing medical oncologists that routinely share their academic interests and clinical trial needs in an effort to assist us in promoting Canada's oncology clinical research capabilities.

Unique applications:

The Reverse Feasibility Program is Scimega's collaborative network of Canada's top-performing medical oncologists that routinely share their academic interests and clinical trial needs. Having these privileged relationships enables us to help U.S. sponsors to significantly speed up their site start-up times and overall patient recruitment, while our deep oncology expertise guarantees top quality data that is critical to making effective go/no-go drug development decisions.

Success stories:

Situation:
A U.S. biotech had a Phase I/II study looking at a novel pathway inhibitor in combination with a popular EGFR inhibitor in CRC. The trial had been ongoing for 7 months at 13 sites in the U.S. The target to end recruitment was by December 2012.

Challenge:
Scarcity of treatment naive subjects (comparator drug used in 1st line in the U.S.) resulted in slow enrollment (0.18 patients per month). They hired a well-known U.S. Site Management Organization to no avail.

Reverse Feasibility Approach:
Scimega introduced the sponsor to select Reverse Feasibility Champions and confirmed investigator interest (comparator arm was approved but not yet reimbursed by payers for 2nd line treatment).

Results:
48-hours later we had confirmed five sites
2-weeks later we had 11 sites to choose from
The sponsor selected the top 8
Time from CPS to IRB approval = 11.2 weeks
Time from IRB to Site Initiation Visits = 3 weeks (1st site in 6 days)
Enrollment: 0.42 patients per site per month.

End of Study was completed 3 months ahead of schedule and two additional programs have since been awarded for expansion to Canada.
