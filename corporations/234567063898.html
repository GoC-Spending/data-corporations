Service Name:
Marketing and Sales Management Services
Keybo is a Canadian marketing and sales management company. Our mandate is to provide our clients with a level of service that will result in measurable increases in revenues, customers and/or markets.

You could think of us as a contracted Director of Marketing and Sales or a virtual marketing & sales department or a temporary placement agency filling executive level sales and marketing management positions.

Not all companies are large enough to afford or wish to pay for a full time marketing executive. As a planner, coach and facilitator, they may only be needed on a short term, part-time or project basis. Keybo can assist these businesses.

Other businesses may be looking at expanding into Canada but may not want the overhead or have the time to initially establish and staff a new office. Keybo can assist these businesses.

Most business owners or executives have the knowledge to do what Keybo does. We simply have the time to focus on the marketing and sales aspect of your business without the distraction of its day to day operational needs.

We also go beyond offering consulting work. There is lots of advice out there for you. We assist with the implementation of our recommendations rather than submit a report that will sit in a desk drawer. Our fee structure is also geared on producing results rather than reports.

If your firm requires help at expanding your business, increasing revenues and adding new customers, let us know your specific needs to see if we can assist you.

Keybo's services can be broken down into these key areas:

Marketing Management. This type of assignment is suitable for a new business, a company looking to open an office or establish distribution in a new market or a firm requiring a total revamp of their marketing strategies. Services can include:

Market research and the development of a marketing strategy or plan
Establishing new sales channels and Channel Management
Development of pricing programs
Implementation of the Marketing Plan
Creation of a marketing message, presentation content and marketing material
Establishing warehousing and distribution
Sales Management
Promotion Management
Sales Management. This type of assignment is suitable for a company requiring a Sales Manager on a part time or short-term basis. Targeted results include increasing the number of contacts, improving relationships with existing customers, improving closing rates (contacts to sales ratios) and enhancing the presentations delivered. Services can include:

Sales Management Auditing
Development of a New Hire Training Manual (see sample outline)
Sales training (Between Nice to Meet You and Thank you for the Business")
Development of compensation plans that delivers a results-motivated sales team
Call scripting and presentations based on prospects hot-buttons and common needs
Competitive intelligence research
Territory structure and management
Contact management and a "keep in touch" systems.
Sales Management Auditing. This type of project is suitable for companies interested in a report of their sales operations with recommendations for improvements that increase the performance of the sales reps. Services can include.

Research of your business offering, customers, operations, non-customers and competition to uncover what is unique to your firm and what your prospective clients are interested in (their hot buttons)
Travel with sales reps to understand your sales cycle, presentation methodology, the common objections occurring, the reasons for win/lost business and the skill set & motivations of the sales reps.
Uncover the hidden value of your offering and ensure its conveyance to prospective customers.
Review sales presentation materials, scripts and tools used and recommend improvements.
Assess the productivity of the sales team (contact & time management) and recommend performance improvements from a procedural and/or motivational standpoint.
Study compensation for its competitiveness at attracting quality professionals and for its ability to drive overachieving performance
Study existing sales training and make recommendations for improvements or deliver a new training program (see sample).
Promotions Management. This assignment is suitable for any business looking to improve the results in creating awareness of their company, special offers, events, etc. or to increase the demand for their products/ services. Assistance can include:

Research your business offering, customers, operation, lost customers and competition to uncover your uniqueness and your prospects real needs.
Study your products/services, pricing and distribution strategies to uncover any inconsistencies.
Analyze current and past promotions to uncover trends and the reasons for both successes and failures.
Develop a marketing message.
Study and recommend promotional mediums and marketing materials, based on a budget and the research.
Request a free promotional planning workbook at www.keybo.ca.


New Product Management. This assignment is suitable for businesses looking to expand their business with the introduction of new products or services. Assistance can include:

Search for additional complimentary products/services to add revenue to existing customers
Manage the new product development process (from a marketing perspective)
Explore re-positioning or re-packaging of existing products for improved sales or new markets
Explore re-packaging existing offerings
Acquisitions. This assignment is suitable for businesses looking to expand their offerings or enter new markets by way of acquisitions. Assistance can include:

Development of selection criteria
Development of investment guidelines and valuation methodology
Sourcing an intermediary to undertake the acquisition search
Channel Management. This assignment is suitable for businesses looking to explore new markets or using new distribution methods to increase sales. Assistance can include:

Review and explore new channels of distribution  dealers, resellers, distributors, agents, exporters, OEM, www, etc.
Development of pricing models for the channel(s) that ensure positive returns, consistent pricing and motivating margins.
Optimization of channel performance through incentive programs, co-op marketing, training, etc.

Unique applications:

6 ways we can:

expand your business, increase revenues and add more customers.

In today's competitive market you are either growing your business or preparing to go out of business. There is no room for complacent companies in most industries.

While there are very few quick fixes for stagnant results, to grow revenues (and profits) you either sell more products/services to existing customers and/or add more customers. That is pretty simple but how do you do it? Here are 6 ways.

Add/modify your channels of distribution.

Are there more or alternate ways that your product or service can be sold?

The value in adding or developing a sales channel is that you expand the number of contacts you can make with prospective customers. Even hiring your first sales person is the start of expanding your channels of distribution.

Not all channels are suitable for every business but below is a list of alternates that can be explored.

Employed Sales Reps
Sales Agents
Dealers or Resellers
Add a Location
Distributors
Exporters
OEMs (make your offering part of another)
Web Sites
Affiliates or a Referral Network
Add products or services.

If you are in retail or distribution, searching for complimentary products/services that your existing customers would buy is a good way to add revenues. Manufacturers can either develop a new product or look for ways to re-position or re-package an existing product.

Improve your sales skills.

This seems simple but if you can make more and better customer or prospective customer contacts, your revenues and customer base will grow.

Selling happens through a variety of mediums including advertising, letters, face-to-face, phone or via the www. Through each of these mediums you can always improve in any of the below:

Targeting prospects
Reaching more prospects
Delivering a stronger message
Understanding the prospects needs, wants and desires
Sales tools - presentations, brochures, etc.
Sales training - product knowledge, competition, sales cycles, personal development
Keep in touch and contact management system
Improve or develop your total marketing strategy.

Marketing is everything about your company because everything affects whether a prospective customer wants to deal with you or an existing customers wants to continue to give you their business.

Many business owners say they don't do any marketing. The fact that they have customers shows that they do some marketing, even if it is treating their customers better than anyone else can.

If you are looking for better sales results, maybe it is time to expand your marketing efforts.

Developing a marketing plan takes some effort. All 4 Ps (Products, Pricing, Place of Distribution and Promotions) have to mesh. Download our free Promotional Planning Workbook

Promote more effectively.

Promotion is just one of the 4 Ps of marketing but it is the one requiring the most creativity. Don't confuse promotion with advertising, as advertising is only one option of promoting your business, its products/services or a special offer/event.

For more details on alternative promotions, download our free Promotional Planning Workbook.

Make acquisitions.

Acquiring companies is a fast way of entering new markets and/or adding more products, services or customers. Acquisitions could be considered adding a channel of distribution but the skills required to buy a business are much different than adding a branch or distributor.

How do you value a business, how do you pay for it, how do you structure a sale and how do you integrate two companies are some of the issues to deal with.

Key / Major clients:

Established the Canadian Dealer Channel for Rochester NY based Xerox Corporation (4 year assignment).
Established Canadian distribution and tech support for San Diego based Internet Magic (2 year assignment).
Contracted by a Merger and Acquisition specialist to act as the Director of Marketing and Sales through an aggressive growth stage (6 month assignment).
Developed a household organizer product line called Recyclist, established manufacturing, distribution and an eventual licensing arrangement (2.5 year project).
Authored the self-help program Inventing and Marketing New Products. This program was published in Canada and promoted through seminars, advertising and media appearances.
Established a Toronto based office technology reseller to offer sales and service. The business was sold after 4 years.
Established Canadian distribution and tech. support for New Jersey based software developer Wordcraft (1 year assignment).
Developed a fundraising program called Earthly Savings for two Toronto based environmental groups
