Company Profile


PARENT COMPANY

SNC-Lavalin, established in 1911, is one of the worlds largest engineering and construction firms and a major developer of transportation infrastructure. The company provides total solutions for its clients, offering engineering, procurement, construction, project management, operations, maintenance and financing services to a variety of industries. Its track record demonstrates a proven ability to successfully finance, design, build and deliver large- scale, complex projects. SNC-Lavalins key business sectors are: transportation, hydroelectric and thermal power, mining and metallurgy, oil and gas, chemicals and petroleum, aerospace, defence, environment, agriculture, pharmaceuticals and telecommunications.

SNC-Lavalin is a public company, listed on the Toronto Stock Exchange. The head office in Montreal, Quebec is complemented by established offices in Canada's other major cities. The company also maintains permanent offices in the United States, Africa, Asia, Central and South America, Europe and the Middle East. Annual revenues exceed $6.1 billion, the majority of which is attributable to international projects. The company employs over 20,000 people in more than 30 offices worldwide.

SNC-LAVALIN TRANSPORTATION

SNC-Lavalin builds lasting transportation infrastructure for cities and communities across the globe, including some of the worlds largest and best-known systems. We offer strategic solutions for the planning, design, development and operation of:

 Mass transit systems and railways
 Highways and bridges
 Airports
 Port and marine facilities

SNC-Lavalins experience in arranging financing and its speedy procurement services ensure it can execute any transportation project for any client, anywhere in the world.

COMMITMENT TO HEALTH, SAFETY AND THE ENVIRONMENT

SNC-Lavalin maintains the highest health, safety and environmental standards. Zero harm is our goal for all of our projects. Pollution prevention and environmental protection measures are integrated into our activities to make sure our projects are environmentally sound and sustainable.

COMMITMENT TO QUALITY

At SNC-Lavalin, we are committed to the quality of our work and to meeting client expectations. The SNC-Lavalin Transportation Division holds ISO 9001:2008 International Standard certification. The scope of this certification includes project management, engineering, procurement, construction and commissioning for rapid transit and other transportation projects worldwide.

Success stories:

CANADA LINE RAPID TRANSIT PROJECT
In 2005, the Canada Line Rapid Transit Project was procured as a $1.9-billion, public-private partnership with a 35-year operations and maintenance component. SNC-Lavalin Inc. submitted the winning bid to design, build, partially finance, and operate the Canada Line, and worked with TransLink, the Cities of Vancouver and Richmond, and the Vancouver International Airport Authority to integrate this 18.5-km and 16-station rapid transit line into unique urban networks using elevated, underground, and at-grade components.
SNC-Lavalins management, design, construction, and operations teams engineered an alignment that used every feasible guideway and station configuration to navigate Metro Vancouvers geometric constraints. A 2.5-km bored tunnel passed under downtown high-rise buildings and underground structures, and integrated three stations into existing buildings. A cut-and-cover tunnel along Cambie Street, which flipped between stacked and side-by-side configurations, resolved vertical and horizontal alignment challenges, while an elevated guideway through Richmond helped shape No.3 Roads new street vision.
The project achieved firsts for Metro Vancouvers rapid transit network: the North Arm Bridge, North Americas first extradosed, cable-stayed bridge; Hyundai-Rotems metro vehicle received its North American introduction and to Automatic Train Control; micropiles supported the 19-metre-deep excavation of Yaletown station. Throughout these technical challenges was SNC-Lavalins sensitivity to impacted communities. An integrated engineering and public communications team led one of the largest public outreach initiatives undertaken in BC.
Successfully delivered on budget and 110 days ahead of schedule, the Canada Line Rapid Transit Project continues to surpass expectations with a system reliability weighing in at 98.9 percent and a capacity to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by an estimated 20 to 27 kilotonnes per year, making the Canada Line an important milestone in shaping Metro Vancouvers transit-oriented future.


TRANSIT EXCELLENCE IN KUALA LUMPUR  A CASE STUDY

The Kuala Lumpur LRT-2 is a 29.4 km, 29-station light rail system, constructed between 1995 and 2000. SNC-Lavalin played a key role in designing and building the system.

Special Achievements:

 Innovative design and construction management for design-build of electrical and mechanical systems:
- trained 500 local construction workers
- established and operated concrete pre-casting plant
- incorporated a pre-cast track slab, which resulted in precise tolerance control, significant reduction of the project's overall schedule and installation of LIM rail at an average rate of 200 track metres per day
- accelerated the main LRT-2 contract schedule by one year and provided significant cost savings

 Awarded four additional design-build contracts for civil infrastructure:
- 29.4 km pre-cast cabletray and walkway
- 500-metre portal and transition tunnel
- Kampung Baru Underground Station
- Ampang Park Underground Station

 Revised geometry and construction methods for the 500-metre transition tunnel, within a difficult profile located between the sensitive river ecosystem and historic buildings

 Reconfigured layout of KG Baru Underground Station, reducing overall station envelope by 30 per cent

 Achieved full client satisfaction and established working relationships still in effect today  currently finalizing a contract to provide light rail vehicles system engineering services

International capital projects:

CANADA LINE RAPID TRANSIT PROJECT, VANCOUVER, BC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin was responsible for the total engineering, procurement and construction (EPC) of an approximately 18 km light rail line with 16 stations linking downtown Vancouver with the international airport in Richmond and Richmond City Centre. InTransitBC, a limited partnership formed by SNC-Lavalin, British Columbia Investment Management Corporation (bcIMC) and the Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec, own and operate the Canada Line through a 35 year concession.

The Canada Line Project has been the recipient of several prestigious awards, including the recent Schreyer Award for most technically innovative project in 2009 from the Association of Consulting Engineering Companies (ACEC)/L'Association des firmes d'ingénieurs-conseils (AFIC) and Canadian Consulting Engineer magazine. The Canada Line was also named as one of the top 100 infrastructure projects in the world for 2009.

WILLIAM R. BENNETT BRIDGE, KELOWNA, BC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin's Okanagan Lake Concession Limited Partnership (OLCLP) was responsible for the total design, construction, financing and operation of the new William R. Bennett Bridge, a floating bridge across Okanagan Lake in Kelowna, British Columbia, under a 30-year concession agreement with the Province of British Columbia. The contract included decommissioning of the existing bridge, which was removed once the new crossing was been built.

The new bridge, completed in the summer of 2008, replaces an aging three-lane structure and is the centerpiece of a revitalized road network in the Okanagan. The five-lane structure relieves congestion along Highway 97, ensuring the safe, efficient movement of people and goods through this growing corridor, and plays a pivotal role in growing the regional economy, particularly the Okanagans tourism industry.

CALGARY LRT EXTENSIONS, ALBERTA, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin has provided trackwork engineering-design services on northwest and northeast extensions to this light rail system in Alberta, Canada, as well as electrical systems engineering and construction management for all extensions (northwest, northeast and south). The 3.6 km South Extension, Phase 1, includes two propulsion power substations and two passenger stations (completed in October 2001). The 2.9 km South Extension, Phase 2, includes two propulsion power substations and two passenger stations (completed June 2004). The 2.7 km Northwest Extension, Phase 1, has two propulsion power substations and one power substation (completed December 2003). The 4.0 km Northwest Extension, Phase 2, has one intermodal station (ongoing). The 3 km Northeast Extension will include a Park and Ride facility, depot, passenger station and substation.

SEA TO SKY HIGHWAY OWNERS ENGINEER, BC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin served as Owner's Engineer on the Sea to Sky Highway Improvement Project, from West Vancouver to Whistler, British Columbia. As such, the company was a key member of the project management team led by the BC Ministry of Transportation. The teams mandate included providing technical advice, design, construction quality management and project management services to ensure the project meets requirements for the 2010 Olympics.

SNC-Lavalin was awarded first prize in the category of Recognition of Excellence in Specialized Services for its work on the project by the BC Ministry of Transportation at the annual Changing Lanes Transportation Conference in February 2004.

This prestigious award from the client showcases the consistently high level of service that SNC-Lavalin's engineers and technicians have so far provided, and will continue to provide until the 2010 Winter Olympic Games.

KUALA LUMPUR LRT-2, MALAYSIA

SNC-Lavalin has provided a variety of services to the development of this system  a 29.4 km light rail line with 24 stations  over the last decade. The company is currently finalizing a contract to provide light rail vehicles system engineering services, including a system study, system engineering, vehicle procurement, testing and commissioning for a fleet expansion and system upgrade. Previously, SNC-Lavalin was a leading component of the Canadian consortium that was awarded a nearly $1-billion contract in the latter part of 1994 to develop the system. SNC-Lavalin provided design-build of E&M systems, as well as design-build of a 500 m tunnel and portal section, two underground stations and precast cabletray and walkway as part of a consortium.

BC FERRIES TERMINAL ASSET MANAGEMENT, BC, CANADA

BC Ferry Services Inc., a newly independent company responsible for the management of British Columbias provincial ferry system, has engaged SNC-Lavalin to manage the construction and maintenance of all 47 of its terminals in British Columbia. The partnership arrangement between BCFS and SNC-Lavalin involves the formation of a new business unit, known as Terminal Asset Management (TAM), consisting of both BC Ferries and SNC-Lavalin personnel.

The TAM group manages an annual program of $40 million in capital construction and $15 million in annual maintenance. By bringing efficiencies and best practices to the BC Ferries Maintenance Group, the TAM team is establishing a comprehensive preventative maintenance program that provides best use of assets. The TAM team is also looking at third-party opportunities for asset management outside of the BC Ferries organization.

HIGHWAY 33, KELOWNA, BC, CANADA

Highway 33, which is classified as a Provincial Secondary Highway, connects Highway 97, Okanagan Highway, at Kelowna, to Highway 3, the Southern Trans Provincial Highway, at Rock Creek, in British Columbia. The project involves the identification and performance of all engineering work required to complete the geotechnical, environmental, traffic, functional and detailed design of approximately 4 km of a four-lane rural arterial undivided highway and associated works along Highway No. 33 to a 70km/h rural arterial undivided standard.

This project is intended to improve the safety and mobility of the two-lane, 60-km/h-posted-speed section of Highway 33 between Muir Road, where the highway already has four lanes, and Gallagher Road, east of where the highway transitions into the 70-90 km/h posted speed rural environment.


MONTREAL METRO, QUEBEC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin lead a consortium responsible for engineering, procurement and construction management work for the 5.2 km extension of metro line 2 to Laval in Montreal. The work involved extending the metro line siting and building three stations (including one intermodal station connecting to the Blainville suburban train line), eight auxiliary structures, an underground garage at the terminal rail tracks of the Montmorency station and a connection to the St-Charles garage.

The first phase of the mandate involved validation and revision of the concepts established during a previous feasibility study, relating to the structures and systems as well as the planned operational procedures. Preliminary plans and specifications, along with a cost estimate at ±10%, were produced at the end of this phase.

The second phase consisted of performing the actual construction work, including the preparation of definitive engineering and architectural plans and specifications, procurement, project and construction management, and delivery of an operational system.

VANCOUVER SKYTRAIN, BC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin has been a key player throughout the development, project management, design and construction of all four completed phases of this 50 km, fully automated light rail system, including conceptual design, preliminary design, public consultation, urban planning, route studies, tender documents, alignment, detailed design of stations and trackwork and management of E&M systems.

ANKARA METRO, TURKEY

SNC-Lavalin planned, financed and negotiated the entire package for the Ankara Metro, leading a Canadian-Turkish consortium through the financing, design, construction, testing and commissioning phases. The projects innovative financial structure is now being modelled in many parts of the world.

MALTA INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT, MALTA

In August 2002, Malta Mediterranean Link consortium, comprising SNC-Lavalin Inc. and two partners, assumed 40% ownership of Malta International Airport plc., concessionaire of Malta International Airport under a 65-year concession agreement. In December 2002, the Malta government reduced its stake by selling 20% of its holding via an IPO. As a result, Malta International Airport plc is today a quoted company on the Malta Stock Exchange. As part of the transaction, SNC-Lavalin has been granted a technical services agreement to assist the airport concessionaire in infrastructure development, the development of its cargo business, and the implementation of a smooth transportation system for passengers travelling between the airport and cruise ships in the Port of Valletta.

VANCOUVER INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT, BC, CANADA

SNC-Lavalin, via its wholly owned subsidiary Pacific Liaicon and Associates Ltd., has served as Owners Representative/ Project and Construction Manager since 1992 for the not-for-profit Vancouver Airport Authority, delivering capital works of over 185,000 m2 of Terminal Facilities, Parallel Runway, Control Tower, Hold Baggage Screening and other special projects.

LUOYANG-SANMENXIA HIGHWAY, CHINA

SNC-Lavalin, as part of a joint venture team that included Henan Provincial Highway Consulting Company from the Peoples Republic of China, provided construction supervision services on an expressway project in Henan Province in China. The Luoyang-Sanmenxia Expressway includes a 136 km, four-lane expressway with fully controlled access, 17.8 km of linking roads and the Yangjia-Baijiazhai Connector, a 6.1 km long Class II highway. SNC- Lavalins scope of work consists of civil (roadworks and structural), electrical, electronic and mechanical works for traffic surveillance, tolling, lighting and communication, buildings for operations and maintenance, service areas, interconnections to the electricity grid and safety facilities.

TURPAN-URUMQI-DAHUANGSHAN HIGHWAY, CHINA

The Turpan-Urumqi-Dahuangshan High Grade Highway Project (TUDH) is a 286 km toll expressway in Xinjiang. The project consisted of civil works, electrical/electronic and mechanical works for traffic surveillance, tolling, communication and lighting, buildings for operations and maintenance and service areas. Due to requirements of the funding agency, SNC-Lavalin was selected to provide a foreign component to the joint Chinese/foreign construction supervision team. Six full-time highway engineers were provided and worked closely with their Chinese counterparts, providing technical advice on all aspects of construction supervision and management, including quality control management, schedule control management, cost control management, claims management and records management.

LUOYANG-SANMENXIA HIGHWAY, CHINA

SNC-Lavalin, as part of a joint venture team that included Henan Provincial Highway Consulting Company from the Peoples Republic of China, provided construction supervision services on an expressway project in Henan Province in China. The Luoyang-Sanmenxia Expressway includes a 136 km, four-lane expressway with fully controlled access, 17.8 km of linking roads and the Yangjia-Baijiazhai Connector, a 6.1 km long Class II highway. SNC- Lavalins scope of work consists of civil (roadworks and structural), electrical, electronic and mechanical works for traffic surveillance, tolling, lighting and communication, buildings for operations and maintenance, service areas, interconnections to the electricity grid and safety facilities.

TURPAN-URUMQI-DAHUANGSHAN HIGHWAY, CHINA

The Turpan-Urumqi-Dahuangshan High Grade Highway Project (TUDH) is a 286 km toll expressway in Xinjiang. The project consisted of civil works, electrical/electronic and mechanical works for traffic surveillance, tolling, communication and lighting, buildings for operations and maintenance and service areas. Due to requirements of the funding agency, SNC-Lavalin was selected to provide a foreign component to the joint Chinese/foreign construction supervision team. Six full-time highway engineers were provided and worked closely with their Chinese counterparts, providing technical advice on all aspects of construction supervision and management, including quality control management, schedule control management, cost control management, claims management and records management.
