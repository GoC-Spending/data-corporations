Company Profile


A service company providing Quality Assessment Management to the
hospitality and transportation industries, food suppliers and
retailers using state of the art computerized Integrated Food and
Services Quality Management (IFSQM©) system.

Medina Foods Inc. - Company Background

Danielle Medina, President and CEO of Medina Foods Inc.,
graduated from Laval University in 1971 with a science degree in
Dietetics, went on to receive a Masters Degree from Université de
Montréal, and in September of 1980 opened Nutri-Centre Medina,
whit the intention of improving the health and well-being of all
Canadians.

The direction of the Company began to materialize with the
development of a nutritional software program that enabled Medina
to provide personalized nutritional evaluations for use in
private consultations, and to employees of large corporations
such as Johnson and Johnson. The program also enabled the
company to conduct nutritional research in the area of infant
nutrition for food companies such as H.J. Heinz, to develop
NUTRI-TEST (nutrition education software) for Uncle Ben's Canada
and Videoway Quebec, NUTRI-ACTION (assessment programs) for the
Coaching Association of Canada and most importantly to launch
NUTRI-CUISINE, a software program for the development and
analysis of recipes and menus aboard Air Canada flights, for
which Medina received 3 international awards. It quickly became
apparent that the stamp of the Company, the Medina Mark of
Quality, was to serve as a symbol of honesty, integrity and a
commitment to excellence in the quality of services provided.

It was the success of this innovative concept that led to both
Medina's expansions into the international market, and the
Company's latest success - IFSQM, a state of the art Integrated
Foods and Services Quality Management program. The new program
extends the quality review to all steps of the process, including
assessments of the logistics of food transportation, production,
maintenance of inventory for equipment and more importantly, food
safety. IFSQM, which ensures that in-flight meals prepared by
caterers around the world and verified in its quality and
conformity to nutritional specification by Medina specialists in
nutrition and quality management, received international
recognition from professional organizations such as IFCA
(International Flight Catering Association), IFSA ( International
Flight Services Association) and the widely distributed Travel
and Leisure Magazine, published by American Express.

Medina's concern for the health and well-being of all Canadians,
coupled with her experiences in the food industry, led to the
creation of Canadian Native Haute Cuisine developed in
conjunction with Oudeehemin Foods Inc., owned in majority by the
Cree Indians of the province of Quebec. The unique cuisine
presented at the World Culinary Olympics in Frankfurt in 1992,
won 8 gold medals, 2 silver medals, and 2 bronze. In 1994,
Medina organized the prestigious banquet of The American Society,
while in 1995, she was asked to cater the G-7 Summit in Halifax.
The success of Canadian Native Haute Cuisine can be attributed,
in no small part, to high caliber of taste and nutrition, backed
by the Medina Mark of Quality .

As proof of her dedication to improve the well being of not only
Canadians, but Canada as well, Danielle, in conjunction with
major Chefs de cuisine such as Paul Bocuse, and the New Brunswick
Government, launched Lobster Nouveau, in early 1993. The aim of
the project was to promote Canadian lobsters in France during the
spring season. This innovative and daring project was such a
success, that experience was repeated. Now, the Atlantic Lobster
carries with it the Medina Mark of Quality , signifying the
strict adherence to criteria concerning the weight, wrapping,
transportation and preparation of lobster. The Medina Mark of
Quality , thus remains a symbol of quality assurance of each and
every product bearing its seal.


VISION

To establish the Medina Mark of Quality as the International
symbol of Quality Assurance and Excellence, translating into the
value-added promise for each of our customers in the hospitality
and transportation industries as well as food retailers and
suppliers.

MISSION

Establish and Promote the Medina Mark of Quality as the
International symbol of Quality Assurance and Excellence,
translating into the value-added promise for each of our
customers in the hospitality and transportation industries as
well as food retailers and suppliers.

Innovate through Research of the quality assessment process and
Development of software for the hospitality and transportation
industries as well as food retailers and suppliers.

Bolster the credibility of the Medina Mark of Quality through the
creation of alliances with recognized international bodies such
as professional organizations and established universities and
promote their involvement in the development and monitoring of
our quality assessment program.

Develop nutrition education software programs to be promoted by
the food industry and telecommunication companies who support
Medina Foods Inc.'s commitment to educate the public on the
importance of high quality nutritional products and their effects
on the health and well being of a people.


Major Achievements

Throughout it's evolution, the Company has consistently projected
a strong image of quality in the development of advanced
technology for the Food Service Industry, through innovative
approaches to quality assessment and in it's offering of other
diverse and unique services to various companies and sectors of
the public. Medina is proud of its major accomplishments over
the last 17 years;
- Three (3) international awards of the food concept NutriCuisine
launched with Air Canada in 1985;

- Its participation in the World Culinary Olympics in 1992 with
the Canadian Native Haute Cuisine which won 7 gold medals, 2
silver and 2 bronze.

- The catering of both the G-7 Summit (1995) and America's
Society Annual Banquet (organization of business leaders in
1994) with Canadian Native Haute Cuisine.

- The introduction of the innovative concept of Lobster Nouveau
in France in 1993 with world leader Chef de Cuisine, Paul
Bocuse;

- In 1992, President and CEO, Ms Danielle Medina, received a
Commemorative Medal for the 125th Anniversary of the
Confederation of Canada, from the Governor General, in
recognition of her numerous and significant contribution to
compatriots, community and to Canada.

- Ms. Danielle Medina, President, CEO of Medina Foods Inc.
received the ``Gloire de L'Escolle`` Medal of Achievement in
1996 from Laval University in recognition of the exceptional
quality shown in the exerciser of her profession.

- In 1996, President and CEO Ms. Danielle Medina was named Quebec
Woman Entrepreneur of the Year.

- Silver Mercury Award winner in 1997 for our Medina Integrated
Food and Services Quality Management (IFSQM©) program presented
at the International Food Catering Association at the annual
meeting in Belgium.

- Integrated Food and services Quality Management (IFSQUM©) :

The success of our Quality Assessment Program is based on four
elements regularity of execution, objectivity in obtaining and
evaluating the data, clear and timely reporting of results, and
a cost efficient process and application. Extensive auditing
experience, in-house training systems and data surveys provide
the back-drop to this program created for the transportation
industries. Clients such as Hilton Canada, CN & CP hotels and
Air Canada are at the base of our experience in the development
of this state of the art Quality Management system.

Innovative
using advanced technology that permits quick and efficient data
collecting, and analysis and communication. Il allows our client
to be close to their products even at a distance as well as
ensuring rapid adjustments essential to the efficacy of the
quality assessment process.

Flexible

Cost Efficient
providing unbiased results, with turn-key infrastructure enabling
us to manage human resources, selection of auditors, planning and
execution of audits on behalf of our clients.

Results Oriented

client receive the results of their quality assessment within 24
hours.

Service Name:
Analyser : FBA© - Food Balance Analyser
Analyser : FBA© - Food Balance Analyser

Nutritional Software

Using research compiled by Median Foods Inc., this recipe
analyser takes up-to-date scientific information on nutrition and
applies it to food products, recipes and menus, to test whether
various nutritional criteria, as well as standards of good taste,
have been met. Our clients are, therefore, assured of receiving
nutritional evaluations and standardization of the highest
quality available for their market needs.

The Food Product Labelling software, for food producers, attached
to the Recipe Analyser allows the food industry to conform itself
to the nutritional data classifications of the food labelling
laws, and regulations , of the United States and Canada.

Development and Commercialization of Value Added Food Products

NutriCuisine is perhaps Medina Foods Inc.'s best known program
related to the recipe analyser. Initially launched on Air Canada
flights in 1985, NutriCuisine is designed with the business
traveller in mind. The menus are based on a balance of nutrients
that are low in fat, salt and sugar, and satisfy the daily
nutritional requirements of airline passengers, as well as
providing a delicious meal. The response from both the public
and the industry has been very positive and appreciative this
concern their welfare.

Service Name:
Lobster Nouveau
Fishery Product :
Medina's Certified Lobster Nouveau, is the most recent project of
Medina Foods Inc. Launched in 1993, the main objective was to
promote Canadian lobsters in France during the spring season. It
was developed in the hopes of becoming a yearly counterpart to
the Beaujolais Nouveau wine, which is a long-awaited event in
Canada each year.

This daring project was such a success that Median Foods Inc.
decided to repeat the experience, but with a new element. Form
now on, the Canadian Atlantic Lobster wears the Medina Mark of
Quality, as assurance to the buyer that strict criteria
concerning the weight, wrapping, transportation and preparation
of the lobster have been strictly adhered to.
