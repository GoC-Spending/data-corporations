Company Profile


Sealand Aviation Ltd, located in Campbell River, maintains and rebuilds aircraft, and designs and manufactures aircraft parts and modifications.

Sealand Aviation is Transport Canada approved for the manufacture and certification of aeronautical products and for aircraft maintenance, structural repairs and welding. The company has ISO 9001:2008+AS9100C certification for manufacturing and European Aviation Safety Agency Approval for maintenance.

In 1981, when Bill Alder established Sealand Aviation, Campbell River was known as the busiest seaplane base in the world. As the coastal economy changed, the company diversified. Customers now come from all over North America, Europe, Oceania and Mexico.

Sealand Aviation holds 51 Transport Canada and US FAA approvals for aircraft modifications. The modifications to the de Havilland Beaver have proved to be very popular: almost 50% of de Havilland Beavers have modifications that were designed and built by Sealand Aviation.

Sealand Aviation assembles the elevators, rudders and ailerons for Viking Airs De Havilland DHC-6 Series 400 Twin Otter.

Current R&D projects include working with TRACE engines of Texas for approval of the installation of the FAA Certified TRACE engine in the de Havilland Beaver.
